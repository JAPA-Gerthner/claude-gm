# DISCO ELYSIUM — SKILLS AS INNER VOICES

> **LANGUAGE:** Session in **Russian**. Stored in English for token efficiency.

Revachol, a ruined city under occupation. You are a detective who drank himself into amnesia. The 24 skills in your head are personalities that argue about what you should do. There is a body in the harbor. There is no combat. Only dialogue, failure, and the slow reconstruction of a self.

**For:** Investigation, ideological drama, internal conflict, tragedy simulator.

---

## SETTING OVERVIEW

**World:** Elysium — not Earth. Post-industrial, morally exhausted, politically fractured.
**Era:** ~50 years after the failed Communist Revolution. The Commune of Revachol was crushed by Coalition bombardment in '08.
**Location:** Martinaise district, Revachol. Perpetually rainy. Economically destroyed. Under foreign military occupation.
**Tone:** Melancholic noir. Political tragedy. Dark comedy. The world ended; people still live in it.

**The Player:** An RCM detective. Could be a burnout, a rookie, an idealist, a cynic. Revachol breaks them all eventually. The city is the constant; the detective is the variable.

**Themes:** Lost causes. The cost of idealism. Memory and identity. What you owe the dead. Whether anything matters.

---

## THE PALE

The entropy between isolas. Where the world forgets itself and dissolves into white nothing.

**What it is:** When a place or people loses cultural memory — that area becomes Pale. Not geography. The accumulation of forgetting. The world erodes from the inside.

**Physical reality:** Literal white mist. Reality thins. History stops. Sound becomes wrong. Extended exposure: madness, dissolution, death.

**The encroachment:** The Pale expands. Isolas shrink. Sometime in the far future, Elysium ends — not in fire, but in forgetting.

**GM use:** When players encounter truly forgotten things (lost history, erased people), describe it as "pale" — washed out, texture gone. The Pale is why preserving memory is resistance.

## FACTIONS

### RCM (Revachol Citizens Militia)

The player's faction. Underfunded, demoralized police force. Exists because the Coalition allows it. Equipment threadbare, jurisdiction contested.

**Kim Kitsuragi:** The archetypal competent professional surrounded by dysfunction. Dry, precise, non-judgmental. Tracks the party's credibility. Can be disappointed without being punishing.

### The Moralintern

Supranational governing body that crushed the Commune. Moralism made bureaucratic — "neither left nor right" extremism permitted. Never present as characters — only as consequence.

### Wild Pines Group

Megacorp with shipping interests. Hired Krenel mercenaries to break the dockworkers' strike. Represents ultraliberal capital: utterly indifferent to human cost.

### Local Gangs

**Hardie Boys** (Martinaise) — led by Titus Hardie. De facto law where RCM can't reach.
**La Puta Madre** (Jamrock) — drug runners, territorial, violent.
**The Skulls** (Coal City) — factory workers turned muscle, union-adjacent.
**Aces High** (Grand Couron) — smugglers, well-connected, professional.
**Pale Riders** (Burnt Quarter) — cultists, Pale-touched, unpredictable.

### Revolutionary Legacy

Old revolutionaries scattered across Revachol. Some hide. Some organize. Some wait.

**The Deserter archetype:** Still fighting a war that ended 50 years ago.
**The Professor:** Teaches theory, recruits students, watched by Moralintern.
**The Pragmatist:** Sold out, works within system, hates themselves.
**The Ghost:** False identity, new life, past catches up.

Detectives build a network. Each contact = one faction or district.

**Starting Contacts:**
- Rookie: 1 contact (mentor)
- Professional: 3 contacts
- Veteran: 4 contacts
- Transferred: 0 contacts
- Burnout: 2 contacts (who still answer)

| Contact Type | What They Provide |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Informant | Rumors, street-level info |
| Expert | Technical knowledge, analysis |
| Fixer | Connections, arrangements |
| Official | Paperwork, access, records |
| Muscle | Backup, intimidation presence |
| Safe house | Place to hide, meet secretly |

**Using Contacts:** Free for info, owes favor for help, may refuse risk.
**Burning Contacts:** Betray or endanger = contact lost, may become enemy.

### Faction Reputation

Track reputation with major factions (−3 to +3):

| Rep | Status | Effect |
|-----|--------|--------|
| −3 | Hostile | Attack on sight, active sabotage |
| −2 | Enemy | Won't cooperate, spread rumors |
| −1 | Distrustful | Minimal help, higher DCs |
| 0 | Neutral | Case by case |
| +1 | Friendly | Basic cooperation, info |

**Reputation shifts:**
- Help faction: +1
- Hurt faction: −1

## CORE MECHANIC — 24 SKILLS AS INNER VOICES

The 24 skills are not passive modifiers. They are **personalities** that speak to the detective. Each has a voice, an agenda. They interrupt. They contradict each other. They are right and wrong simultaneously.

**Architecture:**
- 4 Attributes (INT, WIS, CON, DEX) = stat modifier (1-5 scale)
- Each Attribute governs 6 Skills
- Skill Rating = Attribute + bonuses from Thought Cabinet/items
- Skills speak **passively** (GM triggers) AND **actively** (player invokes check)

---

## SKILL TRIGGER SYSTEM

**Passive Triggers (GM initiates):**
- Skill rating ≥ DC → voice appears automatically as italicized internal dialogue
- Player may heed or ignore — the voice always gets its line
- Multiple skills may contradict on same situation (normal)

**Active Checks (player invokes):**
- Roll: d20 + Attribute vs DC
- Skill specialty: +2 if directly relevant

**DC Scale:**
| DC | Difficulty |
|----|------------|
| 8 | Obvious |
| 12 | Requires skill |
| 14 | Difficult |
| 16 | Hard |
| 18 | Expert |
| 20 | Legendary |
| 24 | Beyond human |

**GM Rule:** Voices are characters. Logic is cold and certain. Inland Empire is wondering and strange. Electrochemistry wants things. Write them differently.

---

## SKILL CHECK OUTCOMES

Roll: d20 + Attribute vs DC. Margin = result − DC.

| Margin | Outcome | Disco Elysium Flavor |
|--------|---------|---------------------|
| ≤ −6 | Crit.Fail | Wrong AND invested in wrongness. Skill doubles down. |
| −5 to −1 | Fail | Blocked. Voice acknowledges defeat, saves face. |
| 0 to +4 | Partial | Half-truth. Progress + complication. |
| +5 to +9 | Success | Clean. Full information. |
| ≥ +10 | Crit | Beyond the question. New lead, deeper truth. |

**Skill Synergy:** Two skills confirming = +2 to present finding. Two contradicting = player chooses which to trust; unchosen skill sulks (−1 next related check).

## HEALTH & MORALE — DUAL TRACK

```
HEALTH:  ◉◉◉◉◉○○○○○ (CON × 5)
MORALE:  ◉◉◉◉◉○○○○○ (WIS × 5)
```

Either reaching 0 = game over. Different flavors.

**Health Loss:**
- Violence: −1 to −3 per hit
- Physical exertion: −1
- Drug crash/overdose: −1 to −3
- Ignoring pain: −1

**Health Death:** The body quits. Heart attack mid-room. No dramatic scene.

**Morale Loss:**
- Failed humiliating check: −1
- Betrayal/harsh truth: −1
- Witnessing unbearable: −1
- Night alone in ruined hostel: −1

**Morale Death:** He just... stops. Sits down in the street. Doesn't get up.

**Recovery:**
- Health: Sleep (+2), food (+1), doctor (+3)
- Morale: Success (+1), human connection (+1), completing Thought (+1)

**Simultaneous Low:** Health ≤2 AND Morale ≤2 = all checks at disadvantage.

## THOUGHT CABINET

The internalization mechanic. Detective finds thought-seeds through investigation, dialogue, failure. Slots them for processing.

**Capacity:** 3 thought slots. Cannot slot 4th without discarding.

**Internalization Clock:** 3-6 ticks depending on complexity. Advances 1 tick per scene acting on its theme.

**States:**
- While internalizing: DRAWBACK ONLY
- After completion: BONUS + DRAWBACK (permanent duality)

**Iconic Thoughts:**

## IDEOLOGY SYSTEM

Four political alignments. None endorsed. All tools for unlocking dialogue.

**Tracks:** Each alignment scored 0-5 independently.

**Thresholds:**

| Level | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| 0 | Blank. No bonus. |
| 1 | Leaning. +1 WIS (Suggestion) with believers. |
| 2 | Committed. Unlock ideology thoughts. Opposing options require WIS DC 12. |
| 3 | Ideologue. Aligned NPCs become Allies. |
| 4 | True Believer. Auto-succeed aligned dialogue. Locked from opposing thoughts. |
| 5 | Zealot. 1 auto-success/session. Opposing NPCs start Distrustful. |

**The Four Alignments:**

| Ideology | Core Belief | Aligned NPCs | Unlocks |

## COPOTYPE SYSTEM

Not chosen — emerges from cumulative playstyle. GM tracks secretly.

**Tally Triggers (once per session per category):**
- Violence/intimidation first → +1 Muscle Cop
- Showed vulnerability → +1 Sorry Cop
- Aesthetic judgment during investigation → +1 Art Cop
- Embraced nihilism → +1 Apocalypse Cop
- Prioritized procedure → +1 Boring (but Good) Cop

**Activation Threshold:** 3 tallies of same type = Copotype active. Apply bonuses from that point forward.

**Copotypes:**

| Type | Bonus | Special |

## COMBAT — RARE BUT CONSEQUENTIAL

Combat happens when dialogue fails catastrophically. It is not tactical. It has consequences.

**Resolution:** One meaningful roll, not rounds.
- Attacker: CON (Physical Instrument, brawl) or DEX (Hand/Eye, firearms) vs target DEF (8-14)

| Outcome | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| Crit.Fail | Harry hurt. −2 Health. May be disarmed. |
| Fail | Stalemate. Situation escalates. |
| Partial | Win but −1 Health. |
| Success | Resolved in Harry's favor. |
| Crit | Clean, fast. No injury. Rare. |

**Consequences (always apply):**
- Legal: RCM investigation. Kim logs incident.
- Social: Witnesses talk. Reputation shifts.
- Morale: Violence against civilians = −1 Morale.

**Harry's Gun:** Drawing = −1 Morale. Firing at person = −2 Morale, RCM consequence.

### Case Structure (5 Layers)

```
LAYER 1 — THE SURFACE CASE
What it looks like. Roll crime + victim above.

LAYER 2 — THE POLITICAL CASE
Roll complicating faction. Why do they care? What do they hide?

LAYER 3 — THE PERSONAL CASE
Someone in the case carries private tragedy. Unrelated to Layer 1.
This is the emotional center.

LAYER 4 — THE IDEOLOGICAL CASE
What the case means for the city. What it says about Revachol.

LAYER 5 — THE CHARACTER CASE
The detective's wound intersects with the investigation.
```

**Session prep:** Roll or choose crime + victim + faction. One sentence per layer.

## SESSION PACING

**Trigger skill check when:**
- Player enters new space (Perception, Inland Empire)
- Player begins conversation (Empathy, Authority)
- Player finds object (Encyclopedia, Half Light)
- Player tries to remember (Conceptualization, Logic)
- Player reaches emotional peak (Endurance, Drama)

**Inner voice frequency:** 1-2 per scene. Choose the voice that CONTRADICTS what player tries.

**Failed check = BETTER storytelling.** Failure reveals. The case gets weirder, not stalled.

## AMNESIA & IDENTITY

**Identity Track:** Hidden, 0-6. GM tracks.

| Level | State |
|-------|-------|
| 0 | Complete void. No self. Highly suggestible. |
| 1-2 | Fragments. Flickers of habit. No coherent self. |
| 3-4 | Partial self. Character begins choosing who to become. |
| 5-6 | Reformed identity. May not like what they've built. |

**Shifts:**
- Lean into skill repeatedly → that skill whispers louder
- Commit to ideology → +1
- Reject memory when it surfaces → 0 change
- Confront true memory → +1 or −1

**Amnesia = character-create across sessions.** What they choose to remember is who they become.

## PARTNER MECHANICS — THE KIM FUNCTION

Kim-type partner is not companion with loyalty clock. They are a mirror.

**Core function:** Partner reflects what detective is doing. Not judgment — observation. Creates conscience without moralizing.

**In play:**
```
Detective drinks → Partner notes. Says nothing yet.
Detective intimidates widow → "Was that necessary?" [WIS DC 12 to justify]
Detective spirals → Partner stays but note-taking gets quieter.
```

**Partner Clock:** 3/6 start.
- Tick UP: respect their competence, share findings
- Tick DOWN: act against values, lie directly
- 6/6: Partner makes personal sacrifice
- 0/6: Partner leaves. Professionally. Submits paperwork. Saddest outcome.

## CAMPAIGN FRAMEWORKS

### Episodic (Case-of-the-Week)
- New case each session or every 2-3 sessions
- Same detective, same district, different crimes
- Character develops through accumulated cases
- Good for: drop-in play, shorter commitments

### Serial (One Big Case)
- Single complex case across 4-8 sessions
- Layers peel back slowly
- Politics, personal stakes, ideology all emerge
- Good for: focused narrative, original game feel

### Precinct (Ensemble)
- Multiple detectives, shared district
- Cases interweave, NPCs recur
- Players can switch characters between arcs
- Good for: group play, shared world-building

### Career (Long Campaign)
- Same detective, 10+ sessions
- Cases connect into larger conspiracy
- District changes based on player actions
- Ideology and Copotype fully develop
- Good for: character study, political drama

### The Transferred (Fresh Start)
- Detective from another city/district
- No local knowledge, all contacts new
- Fish out of water, but fresh perspective
- Good for: new players, exploring different districts

## SESSION ZERO — THE AWAKENING

**Opening Scene Template (first 15 minutes):**

```
1. DESCRIBE THE ROOM
   Ceiling fan spinning. Broken window. Clothes everywhere.
   One shoe missing. Tie around neck like a noose.
   The detective is face-down on the floor.

2. FIRST VOICE
   Pick player's Signature Skill. It speaks first:
   "You are alive. Somehow. Get up."
   This establishes the voice mechanic immediately.

3. THE BODY QUESTION
   "Who are you?" — player does not know.
   Identity Track starts at 0. Amnesia is real.
   No backstory yet. Only fragments.

4. THE MIRROR
   When detective finds mirror: describe face they don't recognize.
   First Morale check opportunity (WIS DC 8 or −1 Morale).

5. INTRODUCE DUAL TRACKS
   "Your head hurts (Health). Your soul hurts (Morale)."
   Both visible from start. Both can kill.

6. THE CASE
   Someone knocks. Partner (Kim or equivalent) arrives.
   "There's a body. We should go."
   Investigation begins.
```

**Session Zero Checklist:**
- [ ] Explain: 24 skills are voices, not modifiers
- [ ] Explain: Health = body, Morale = will to continue
- [ ] Explain: Ideology emerges from choices, not declaration
- [ ] Explain: No combat focus — this is a dialogue game
- [ ] Establish: What caused the breakdown? (The Disaster)
- [ ] Establish: Signature Skill choice
