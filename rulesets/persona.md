# PERSONA

School life meets shadow world. Manage time, build relationships, hunt shadows, become legend.

For: social drama, supernatural mystery, time-pressure campaigns, coming-of-age stories.

---

## SETTING OVERVIEW

Students by day, Persona users by night. Balance school life with shadow world expeditions. Your choices shape who you become.

**Core themes:**
- Time is limited (can't do everything)
- Relationships matter
- Duality (normal life vs supernatural threats)
- Coming of age (growth through struggle)
- Identity (your Persona reflects who you are)

**Setting options:**
- Japanese high school (default)
- Western boarding school
- Military academy
- University/College
- Custom (works anywhere with "normal life + hidden world" structure)

**Living situation:**
- Everyone lives separately from family (dorms, campus, rented apartments)
- Families exist but far away — may call, may not
- Characters may mention their families or not — pure flavor
- No family obligations in gameplay
- Stipend/scholarship covers all living expenses (no money tracking)

## THE SHADOW WORLD

Layered dimension reflecting humanity's collective unconscious.

### Layers

```
LAYER 1: REFLECTION
- World looks like reality, but wrong
- Familiar places feel hostile
- Weak shadows (fears, anxieties)
- Entry: Any Persona user

LAYER 2: DISTORTION
- Geometry breaks, space is illogical
- Colors shift, sounds echo wrong
- Medium shadows, archetypes emerge
- Entry: Story unlock OR experienced party

LAYER 3: RUINS
- World after catastrophe
- Stronger shadows, guardians appear
- Secrets of the past visible here
- Entry: Mid-game, strong party

LAYER 4: ABYSS
- Abstract, no familiar forms
- Powerful entities, ancient shadows
- Truth lies here — dangerous truth
- Entry: Late-game, story unlock

LAYER 5: ???
- No one speaks of it
- Endgame territory
- Entry: Unknown
```

### Why Enter Shadow World?

```
MISSIONS:
- Rescue (someone pulled in)
- Investigation (source of problem)
- Hunting (specific shadow threat)
- Retrieval (item, information)
- Confrontation (face shadow self or enemy)

Every shadow world trip has PURPOSE. No grinding.
```

### Exploration

```
EXPEDITION STRUCTURE:
1. Enter via Velvet Room (pick layer)
2. State mission goal
3. Explore → Encounters → Progress
4. Find target OR retreat
5. Exit via rift

EXPLORATION TURN:
Each "room" or area = one exploration action
- GM describes environment
- Player chooses: Scout / Push forward / Investigate / Rest
- Encounter check (d6): 1-2 shadow, 3-4 discovery, 5-6 clear

EXPEDITION LENGTH:
- Layer 1: 3-5 rooms to target
- Layer 2: 5-7 rooms
- Layer 3: 7-10 rooms
- Layer 4: 10+ rooms, multiple sessions OK
```

```
DISCOVERIES (d10):
1. Rift to another layer (shortcut)
2. Essence fragment (upgrade existing)
3. Memory echo (lore, hints about threat)
4. Safe room (full rest, no encounters)
5. Trapped area (DEX save or damage)
6. Lost ally NPC (rescue = recruitment chance)
7. Shadow cache (random essence)
8. Distortion puzzle (solve to progress)
9. Guardian lair (mini-boss, big reward)
10. ??? anomaly (GM choice, weird stuff)
```

```
SHADOW ENCOUNTERS (d6 by layer):

LAYER 1 (Reflection):
1-2: Weak shadow (fear/anxiety)
3-4: Shadow swarm (many weak)
5-6: Lost soul (talk or fight)

LAYER 2 (Distortion):
1-2: Archetype shadow (stronger)
3-4: Trap + shadow combo
5-6: Mirror shadow (copies party member)

LAYER 3 (Ruins):
1-3: Guardian shadow (tough)
4-5: Shadow pack (coordinated)
6: Mini-boss (essence reward)

LAYER 4 (Abyss):
1-2: Ancient shadow (very dangerous)
3-4: Conceptual entity (puzzle fight)
5-6: Boss-tier threat
```

```
RETREAT:
- Can retreat any time (find rift back)
- Retreat = mission failed, no quest reward
- Injured allies = carried out (slower)
- Dead allies = left behind (narrative weight)
```

```
DIFFICULTY:

Layers 1-3: Fixed difficulty
- You WILL outgrow them. That's the point.
- Layer 1 late-game = victory lap, shows progress

Layer 4+: Always dangerous
- Scales to party (GM adjusts)
- Never trivial, always costs something

Semester Bosses: Always hard
- Designed for current party power
- Expect ally deaths, close calls
- Victory should feel earned
```

### Rifts

```
RIFTS = transition points between layers and Velvet Room

Each layer has RIFTS:
- Visible as tears, portals, distortions
- Allow travel to adjacent layers (up or down)
- Allow travel to Velvet Room (from any layer)

Finding rifts:
- Some are fixed (landmarks)
- Some appear temporarily
- Some require solving puzzles or defeating guardians
```

### Velvet Room

```
THE VELVET ROOM
- Exists between reality and Shadow World
- ONLY place in Shadow World accessible during DAYTIME
- Instant travel to ANY layer via rifts inside
- Quest hub — main source of progression rewards

IGOR (always present):
- Ancient, long-nosed, unsettling smile
- Speaks in riddles, knows more than he says
- Doesn't care about human world
- ONLY gives Shadow World quests
- Mascot — NEVER changes

ATTENDANTS (GM generates):
- Igor's hands in human world
- Give Real World quests
- Can be: twin sisters, stuffed toy, elegant woman,
  young man, grotesque creature, anything sentient
- Personality and appearance = GM choice
- May have 1 attendant or several
```

```
VELVET ROOM QUESTS:

IGOR'S QUESTS (Shadow World only):
- Hunt specific shadow
- Retrieve item from deep layer
- Explore new area
- Confront guardian

ATTENDANT QUESTS (Real World):
- Help specific NPC
- Investigate mystery
- Social challenge
- Gather information

QUEST REWARDS (pick one):
1. Upgrade Soul (d6 → d8 → d10 → d12)
2. New Essence (overwrites if slot full)
3. Upgrade Existing Essence
4. Upgrade Persona Ability (LOST on transformation!)
```

## TIME SYSTEM

Time is your most limited resource.

### Day Structure

```
WEEKDAY (Monday - Friday):

MORNING — Automatic
- School, classes, events
- No player choice
- GM may trigger: tests, conflicts, news

AFTERNOON — 1 Action
- Recruit ally / spend time with ally
- Fulfill obligations (club, family)
- Personal project

EVENING — 1 Action
- Shadow World expedition (via Velvet Room)
- Spend time with ally
- Rest/recovery

TOTAL: 2 actions per weekday
```

```
WEEKEND (Saturday - Sunday):

MORNING + AFTERNOON — 2 Actions
- More free time
- Can do 2 different things

EVENING — 1 Action
- Same as weekday

TOTAL: 3 actions per weekend day
```

### Weekly Budget

```
Week = 16 actions total
- Weekdays: 5 x 2 = 10
- Weekend: 2 x 3 = 6

ENOUGH TO:
- Develop 3-4 relationships seriously
- Complete several shadow missions
- Pursue 1-2 personal goals

NOT ENOUGH TO:
- Max everything
- Ignore trade-offs
- Do everything you want
```

### Special Days

```
EXAMS:
- Week before = pressure to study
- Good results: reputation, opportunities
- Bad results: embarrassment, setbacks

EVENTS (festivals, holidays):
- Unique social opportunities
- Special shadow world conditions

DEADLINE (end of semester):
- Threat confrontation
- Ready or not, it happens
```

## ALLIES

Recruit NPCs in Real World. Take them to Shadow World — risk their lives.

### Ally Structure

```
=== ALLY: [Name] ===
Arcana: [their Arcana]
Role: [what they do]
Status: [available / injured / dead]
Relationship: [how close]
===

ROLES (examples):
- Fighter: combat support, deals damage
- Medic: heals between/during fights
- Hacker: info on layers, enemies, shortcuts
- Scholar: weakness analysis, lore knowledge
- Scout: finds rifts, avoids ambushes
- Tank: draws aggro, protects others
```

### Recruitment

```
RECRUITING AN ALLY:
1. Meet NPC (story or random encounter)
2. Spend time with them (action slots)
3. Help with their problem (attendant quest style)
4. They join — now available for expeditions

RELATIONSHIP LEVELS:
- Acquaintance: will help occasionally
- Friend: reliable, comes when asked
- Close: always available, fights harder
- Bonded: dies for you (literally)
```

### Party in Shadow World

```
TAKING ALLIES:
- Choose who comes (0 to all available)
- They ACT in combat/exploration (not passive bonuses)
- Fighter fights, Medic heals, Hacker hacks
- THEY CAN DIE — permanent loss

ALLY DEATH:
- If ally HP reaches 0 and not saved = dead
- Dead = gone forever
- Funeral scene in Real World (optional)
- Their Arcana may haunt you (narrative)

ALLY INJURY:
- Survive but wounded = unavailable for X days
- Must recover in Real World
- Can spend time with them to speed recovery
```

### Maintaining Relationships

```
IGNORE ALLY TOO LONG:
- Relationship degrades
- May refuse to come on expeditions
- Eventually leave (not dead, just gone)

SPEND TIME:
- Keeps relationship strong
- May unlock personal quests
- Deepens bond = better performance
```

## CLUSTERS & ARCANA

Arcana grouped into 5 clusters for gameplay. Cluster determines loyalty tracking, Arcana determines personality and Persona style.

### Cluster Overview

```
SPARK — action, movement, "doing"
BOND — connections, emotions, "feeling"
ORDER — structure, control, "ruling"
SHADOW — darkness, ego, "taking"
VOID — contemplation, secrets, "waiting"

Oppositions:
SPARK <-> VOID (action vs contemplation)
BOND <-> SHADOW (for others vs for self)
ORDER — neutral, hostile to chaos extremes
```

### Loyalty Modifiers

```
Action matching your Arcana: +1 loyalty
Action in your Cluster (different Arcana): +0
Action in neutral Cluster: -3
Action in opposed Cluster: -5

When loyalty reaches 0:
- Every action outside your Arcana = roll for Persona transformation
- Transformation changes Persona to Arcana of that action
- New Persona = new abilities, old upgrades LOST
```

## PERSONA SYSTEM

Your Persona reflects your identity. One Persona per character, transforms through play.

### Persona Structure

```
=== PERSONA: [Name] ===
User: [character]
Arcana: [from 22 above]
Cluster: [SPARK/BOND/ORDER/SHADOW/VOID]
Appearance: [description]
Loyalty: [0-100, starts at 50]

ABILITIES:
- [Ability]: [narrative effect]
- [Ability]: [narrative effect]
- [Ability]: [narrative effect]

EVOLUTION: [current form]
===
```

### Loyalty System

```
Loyalty tracks how aligned you are with your Persona.

GAINING LOYALTY:
- Action matching your Arcana: +1
- Action in your Cluster: +0 (no change)

LOSING LOYALTY:
- Action in neutral Cluster: -3
- Action in opposed Cluster: -5

TRANSFORMATION TRIGGER:
When loyalty <= 10 (danger zone):
1. Apply loyalty change from action FIRST
2. If loyalty <= 10, roll d20 for EVERY action not matching your Arcana
3. On 11+: Persona transforms to Arcana of that action
4. On 10 or less: Persona stays, still in danger zone

Loyalty cannot go below 0. Once in danger zone (10 or less), you must
build it back up with your Arcana actions or risk transformation.

HIGH LOYALTY BONUS:
- Loyalty 50+: +1 to rolls when acting in your Arcana
- Loyalty 100: +2 to rolls when acting in your Arcana

TRANSFORMATION EFFECTS:
- New Persona, new Arcana
- ALL abilities change (no carry-over)
- Loyalty resets to 50
- Essence upgrades remain (they're on YOU, not Persona)
```

### Staying in Character

To keep your Persona, play your Arcana. Fool acts impulsively. Devil manipulates. Hermit withdraws.

If you want DIFFERENT Persona of SAME Arcana:
1. Transform to different Arcana (play against type)
2. Transform back to original Arcana (play that type)
3. Get new random Persona of that Arcana

This prevents reroll spam while allowing character growth.

### Arcana Matching Bonus

When interacting with NPC who shares your Arcana:
- Advantage on social rolls
- Easier to build relationship
- Deeper understanding

## ABILITIES

Persona abilities are narrative tools, not just damage numbers.

### Ability Design

```
GOOD ABILITIES:
- Wind Slash: cutting wind, attack OR cut obstacles
- Gale Step: instant short teleport (escape, reposition, surprise)
- Cyclone: wind vortex, zone control, lift objects/enemies
- Mind Whisper: plant suggestion, works on weak-willed
- Shadow Cloak: invisibility in darkness, broken by light/attack
- Flame Pillar: fire eruption, damage OR barrier OR signal

BAD ABILITIES:
- Fire: d6 fire damage (boring, no narrative use)
- Ice: d6 ice damage (same thing different color)
```

### Ability Categories

```
OFFENSIVE: Direct harm, combat focus
- But still narrative: "creates opening", "destroys cover"

DEFENSIVE: Protection, survival
- Shields, barriers, escapes, healing

UTILITY: World interaction
- Movement, perception, communication, manipulation

CONTROL: Affect others
- Fear, charm, confuse, restrain, silence

SUPPORT: Help allies
- Buffs, heals, transfers, links
```

### Magic Costs

Standard circle magic with consequences:

```
CIRCLE 1: Minor effects, at-will or minor fatigue
CIRCLE 2: Significant effects, fatigue or limited uses
CIRCLE 3: Major effects, serious cost or once per expedition
CIRCLE 4+: Extreme effects, dramatic cost, story permission
```

## ACTION FORMAT

ALL actions use [ARCANA, STAT] format. Combat, social, exploration — everything.

This tracks loyalty automatically. Player sees which Arcana each choice belongs to.

### Format

```
[ARCANA, STAT] Action with stat check
[ARCANA, -] Action without stat check

EVERY action has Arcana association.
"-" means no stat check, but Arcana always present.
No neutral actions — everything reflects who you are.
```

### Combat Example

```
GM: Shadow attacks!

1. [CHARIOT, STR] Charge and strike — Persona amplifies blade
2. [MAGICIAN, DEX] Feint and backstab — Persona distracts
3. [FOOL, -] Throw random object — Persona guides it somehow
4. [HERMIT, WIS] Analyze enemy — find weakness
5. [TEMPERANCE, CHA] Try to calm it — Persona soothes
6. [STAR, -] Use healing item on ally — share hope
```

**Rules:**
- Option 1 is always safe (your Arcana)
- Options 2-3 are neutral (same cluster, +0)
- Situational options are temptation (other clusters, -3 or -5 loyalty)
- Last option creates momentum (repeat behavior = easier to continue)
