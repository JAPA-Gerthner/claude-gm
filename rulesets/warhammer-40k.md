# WARHAMMER 40,000 — IRON WILL

---

## PITCH

The Imperium is not civilization. It is a machine.
A machine built on faith, fear, and the corpse of a god.

Control crushes.
The Warp devours.
Entropy waits.

You serve between them.
Every order costs you.
Every power corrupts you.
Every day grinds you down.

The question is not "who will you become?"
The question is: how long can you hold?

---

## BASE SYSTEM

Uses gm-skill.md core mechanics:
- d20 + stat vs DC (margin-based: Crit.Fail / Fail / Partial / Success / Crit)
- Six stats: STR, DEX, CON, INT, WIS, CHA
- Clocks, Heroism, Companions, Abilities — all from base
- Writing style, response format, player rules — all from base

**This ruleset adds:** Three Forces, Scars, Mission Cycle, Rank, Warp-integrated psychic powers, faith as Control tool, 40K-specific content.

**Do not duplicate base mechanics.** If gm-skill covers it, use it as-is unless this ruleset explicitly overrides.

---

## THREE FORCES

Three forces grind every soul in the galaxy. Every person, faction, world exists somewhere in this triangle.

```
        CONTROL
       /       \
      /   YOU    \
     /             \
  WARP ————————— ENTROPY
```

Each force: 0–10 scale.
Starting total across all three: max 4.

**In 40K, all three forces are hostile.** There is no good axis. Control is the Imperium's boot. Warp is Hell reaching in. Entropy is the slow death of meaning. You survive between them.

---

## CORE RULE: ONE SHIFT PER SCENE

Per scene or encounter, apply at most ONE base Force shift.

```
Choose the most significant driver:
- how the problem was solved
- what the character chose under pressure
- the dominant method (obedient / forbidden / passive)

BASE: +1 Force (standard)
      +2 Force (major outcome)
      +3 Force (catastrophic — rare, GM only)

INACTION on a pressing matter: +1 Entropy
```

### Amplifiers

The following can increase the scene's shift by +1 (max total +2 unless catastrophic):

```
- Killing a sentient being (if central to the scene)
- Psychic power as the primary solution
- Extreme methods (Exterminatus-level thinking, torture, sacrifice)

If multiple apply: still cap at +2 total.
GM picks the dominant narrative cause.
```

Target pacing: ~2-5 Force per session. Critical thresholds in 2-4 sessions.

---

## THREE CURRENCIES

The game has exactly three costs. They do not overlap.

```
FORCE     = strategic price (long-term erosion)
            "What's breaking you?"

CONDITION = tactical price (immediate survival)
            "Can you keep fighting?"

FORTUNE   = insurance (limited, spend to avoid the above)
            "Do you burn your luck?"
```

Every significant choice costs one of these. If you can't identify which — the action is free or trivial.

---

## PUSH

Before any roll — declare Push. Choose your price:

```
PUSH (CONTROL): +1d4 to the roll
                +1 Control
                −1 to next independent action
                You fall back on training, doctrine, the Imperial way.

PUSH (WARP):    +1d6 to the roll
                +1 Warp
                Something in the Warp notices.
                You reach for forbidden power. Something reaches back.
```

Both cost something now AND later. Choice is real.

Cannot Push both ways on same roll.
Psychic powers and faith are Pushes — follow same rules.

# PART I: THE THREE FORCES

---

## CONTROL

The Imperium's grip. Faith, discipline, obedience, hierarchy.

```
BENEFITS:
+ authority (Control-aligned factions trust you)
+ resources (requisition, orders, backup)
+ discipline (advantage on morale, group tactics)

COSTS:
- freedom dies
- individuality erodes
- you become the machine
```

### Control Thresholds

```
0-2: FREE — no effect
3:   LOYAL — +1 to ordered actions, −1 to independent decisions.
     You hesitate before disobeying.
4-5: DEVOTED — advantage on morale. −1 to questioning orders.
     You report deviations instinctively.
6:   ZEALOUS — must report heresy/deviation when detected.
     Failure to report: WIS save DC 14 or +1 Condition.
7:   FANATICAL — −2 CHA (except intimidation), +2 vs Chaos.
     You are feared. Even allies keep distance.
8:   INSTRUMENT — cannot refuse any legitimate order.
     Disobedience: +1 Condition. Identity = rank.
9:   HOLLOW — you don't remember why. You just obey.
     −3 CHA. No independent decisions without WIS DC 16.
10:  SERVITOR — NPC. The machine claims you.
     Lobotomized by doctrine, not by surgery.
```

### Reducing Control

```
PATH OF DEFIANCE (costly, dangerous):
-1: Disobey direct order for personal conviction
-1: Show mercy where doctrine demands punishment
-1: Extended time outside Imperial authority (1+ week)
-2: Betray your faction for moral reasons
Cannot reduce below current Rank (see Rank section).

PATH OF HERESY (easier, darker):
-1: Accept forbidden knowledge or xenos aid → +1 Warp
    The Imperium's lies crack. Something else fills the gap.
-1: Abandon post, desert, or sabotage Imperial operations → +1 Entropy
    You're free. You're also alone.
-2: Openly defy the Imperium in a way that helps Chaos → +2 Warp
    One master for another.
```

## WARP

The immaterium's touch. Not just for psykers — the Warp presses on everyone.

```
BENEFITS:
+ psychic power (+1 to casting at Warp 3+)
+ forbidden knowledge (advantage on Chaos/Warp lore)
+ at Warp 5+: sense Warp entities within 30ft automatically

COSTS:
- reality breaks
- daemons notice
- mutation, possession, madness

WARP ENVIRONMENT:
Warp-heavy zones (daemon worlds, Warp storms, Eye of Terror proximity,
active rituals, Necron tomb awakening, Tyranid psychic shadow):
  +1 Warp per scene for everyone present (GM discretion).
  The Warp doesn't care if you're a psyker. It presses on all flesh.

WARP AND CONTROL:
Warp 5+: Push (Control) always adds +1 Warp.
  You tried to use faith? The Warp hijacks it.
  Push (Control) at Warp 5+ = the Emperor's light flickers.
Warp 7+: on natural 1 (any roll), GM describes a Warp event.
  Brief. The walls bleed. A shadow moves wrong. A word appears.
Warp 9+: in stressful moments, WIS save DC 16 or partial possession.
  One action, GM's choice. You watch yourself do something inhuman.
```

### Warp Thresholds

```
0-2: SHIELDED — Emperor's light, no effect
3:   TOUCHED — bad dreams, whispers. −1 WIS saves vs Warp.
     GM rolls mutation check (d20: 18+ = mutation)
4-5: MARKED — minor physical signs (concealable).
     +1 casting, −1 WIS saves. Daemons notice you.
6:   BLEEDING — reality thin around you.
     On any natural 1: roll on Minor Perils table.
     Mutation check: 14+ = mutation.
7:   TORN — involuntary psychic events.
     WIS save DC 14 in stressful situations or Warp bleeds through.
8:   POSSESSED-RISK — gain major mutation if not already.
     −2 CHA. In Warp-heavy zones: WIS DC 16 or brief possession.
9:   DAMNED — Chaos gods actively court you.
     GM offers dark bargains. Refusing costs +1 Condition.
10:  ASCENDED/SPAWN — Daemon Prince (WIS DC 20) or Chaos Spawn.
     Character ends either way. Roll new character.
```

### Reducing Warp

```
PATH OF FAITH (costly, painful):
-1: Extended penance and prayer (weeks, not days)
-1: Pilgrimage to holy site
-1: Act of extreme faith (martyrdom-level sacrifice)
-2: Inquisitorial sanctioning (rare, excruciating: +2 Condition)
-1: Mechanicus purity rites (tech-heresy risk)
Cannot reduce below mutation count.

PATH OF SACRIFICE (easier, darker):
-1: Transfer Warp taint through ritual sacrifice
    Victim dies or gains permanent madness/mutation.
    +1 Entropy. The screaming fades. So does something inside you.
-1: Destroy someone else's soul to cleanse your own (psyker ritual)
    Victim is gone — no afterlife, no Emperor's light. Just void.
    +1 Entropy. You feel cleaner. You shouldn't.
-2: Offer a powerful soul to a rival Chaos entity
    Victim consumed. You're playing gods against each other.
    +2 Entropy, +risk: d20, on 1-4 the entity wants MORE.

The Warp doesn't cleanse. It transfers. Someone always pays.
```

## ENTROPY

The slow death. Bureaucracy, meaninglessness, compassion fatigue, the grinding reality of 40K.

```
BENEFITS:
+ numbness (+1 CON saves at Entropy 3+)
+ at Entropy 5+: ignore Condition 1 AND 2. Pain doesn't register.
+ at Entropy 7+: immune to Fear, Terror, Intimidation, morale effects.
  Nothing reaches you. Nothing can.
+ at Entropy 8+: don't need sleep. You just... continue.

COSTS:
- caring stops
- initiative dies
- you become another casualty statistic

Entropy = strongest defense in 40K. Price = you stop being human.
```

### Entropy Thresholds

```
0-2: FRESH — still feels things
3:   TIRED — −1 CHA in emotional contexts.
     Sleep doesn't help. Food tastes like nothing.
4-5: WORN — advantage vs fear/horror. Cannot feel joy.
     Companions notice your distance. Clock penalty −1.
6:   HOLLOW — immune to Fear. −2 CHA, −1 WIS.
     Names blur. Faces merge. Another day.
7:   SPENT — immune to morale effects. Allies are "assets."
     You forget why you're fighting. The mission is enough.
8:   GHOST — don't need sleep. Half movement.
     You are still moving. No one knows why. Including you.
9:   EMPTY — −3 CHA. No emotional response. Allies may leave.
     You've seen a million die. What's one more?
10:  NUMBER — character functionally dead inside.
     Another casualty. Roll new character.
```

### Reducing Entropy

```
PATH OF CONNECTION (costly, rare):
-1: Genuine emotional connection (rare — GM judges authenticity)
-1: Create something meaningful (not destroy — build, save, protect)
-1: Extended rest in safe community (weeks — where does that exist in 40K?)
-2: Near-death experience that reignites will to live
-2: Someone risks everything to reach you (GM-triggered, not player-initiated)
    Works even above Entropy 7. The numb are saved by those who still care.

PATH OF VIOLENCE (easier, darker):
-1: Consume life force through rage or dark ritual → +1 Warp
    Violence makes you feel alive. Khorne approves.
-1: Brutalize the weak to feel powerful → +1 Warp
    Their fear reminds you that you exist.
-2: Devour someone's will to live through psychic vampirism or torture → +2 Warp
    Their stolen vitality burns in you. It won't last.

Entropy goes down. Warp goes up. You traded emptiness for hunger.
Hardest force to reduce either way. Entropy is patient.
```

## FORCE INTERACTIONS

```
TRIANGULAR TENSION:
- Fighting Chaos requires faith/discipline → +Control
- Fighting the system requires breaking rules → +Warp or +Entropy
- Doing nothing → +Entropy (always)
- Using forbidden power against Chaos → trades Control for Warp
- Enforcing Imperial law through atrocity → Control AND Entropy

PERMEATION:
Control ↓ → Entropy ↑ (without the system, meaning dissolves)
Warp ↓ → Control ↑ (faith/purity tightens the Imperium's grip)
Entropy ↓ → Control or Warp ↑ (you re-engage, but through what?)

DRIFT:
Characters drift toward one force.
The more you use a force's benefits, the faster it grows.

IN 40K: total forces always increase. Balance is temporary.
A character at 3/3/3 is more doomed than one at 7/1/1.
Specialists survive longer but are more extreme.
```

## CONDITION

Condition is derived from HP. Same as base system, but harsher.

```
HP = CON × 5 + 10

CONDITION THRESHOLDS (% of max HP):
0: OPERATIONAL (76-100%) — no penalties
1: HURT (51-75%) — −1 to rolls
2: WOUNDED (26-50%) — disadvantage on rolls
3: CRITICAL (1-25%) — cannot act without CON/WIS save DC 14
4: DYING (0 HP) — death saves begin

CONDITION DIRECTION:
Condition increases when HP crosses a LOWER threshold.
Condition does NOT decrease when HP is restored.
Only explicit recovery (rest, medicae) reduces Condition.

HEAVY HIT: single hit ≥ 25% max HP → +1 extra Condition
MASSIVE HIT: single hit ≥ 50% max HP → +2 extra Condition
```

### Recovering Condition

```
4→3: immediate medicae (INT DC 16) or powerful intervention
3→2: long rest + medicae attention (INT DC 14)
2→1: long rest + basic care
1→0: short rest in safe location

Without safety: recovery one step slower.
Without medicae: recovery two steps slower.
In 40K, "safe location" is rare. "Medicae" may be a saw and prayers.
```

---

## SCARS

Every time you survive Condition 3+, you carry the proof.

```
SCAR TRIGGER: Each time Condition reaches 3 or higher → gain a Scar.
At Condition 4 (survived death via Fate): automatic Scar, no roll.

5+ Scars = character severely impaired. Still playable, but broken.
7+ Scars = character unplayable. Retired, institutionalized, or mercy-killed.
```

### Scar Table (d12, or GM picks)

```
PHYSICAL SCARS:
1:  Nerve damage — −1 DEX (hand tremor, twitching)
2:  Shrapnel — metal in body, triggers auspex scanners
3:  Limp — −5ft movement, permanent
4:  Partial deafness — −1 WIS (perception)
5:  Lost eye — −1 ranged attacks, advantage detect flanking (paranoia)
6:  Burns — −1 CHA, advantage vs fire fear (seen worse)

MENTAL SCARS:
7:  PTSD trigger — specific stimulus, WIS DC 12 or lose action
8:  Nightmares — disadvantage after interrupted rest
9:  Thousand-yard stare — −1 CHA, +1 to resist Intimidation
10: Vengeful — +1 damage vs specific enemy type, must pursue

SYSTEM SCARS:
11: Bionic replacement — works, but +1 Control (Mechanicus debt, you owe them)
12: Warp scar — +1 Warp permanently, gain minor psychic sensitivity
    You touched something. It touched back.

DUPLICATE: same scar = intensifies.
  Lost eye → other eye damaged (−2 ranged, advantage hearing).
  PTSD → multiple triggers.
  Bionic → more machine (+2 Control total).
```

## FATE & FORTUNE

Life is cheap in the 41st Millennium.

### Fate

```
FATE (permanent, never refreshes):

Conscript/Cultist: 0 (you're already dead)
Guardsman/Civilian: 1
Officer/Specialist: 1
Space Marine/Sister: 2
Inquisitor/Rogue Trader: 2

Spend Fate to avoid death at Condition 4:
- Stabilize at Condition 3
- Gain a Scar (automatic)
- Survive what should have killed you

When Fate is gone — you die for real next time.
A guardsman with 0 Fate hits Condition 4 = dead. No second chances.
That's 40K.
```

### Fortune

```
FORTUNE (per session):

Conscript/Cultist: 1
Guardsman/Civilian: 2
Officer/Specialist: 2
Space Marine/Sister: 3
Inquisitor/Rogue Trader: 3

SPEND TO:
- Reroll any one die (keep second result)
- Add +2 to any roll (declare before result)
- Force enemy to reroll one attack against you
- Reduce incoming Condition by 1

Fortune does NOT cheat death. That's Fate.
Fortune does NOT reduce Force shifts. Forces always grow.
Fortune refreshes each session.
```

## MISSION CYCLE

Structure for each operation. Not every mission has all phases.

```
=== BRIEFING ===
• Receive orders (Control pressure: obey or question?)
• Requisition gear (Rank determines limits)
• Intel briefing (may be incomplete, wrong, or deliberately misleading)
• Squad/retinue assignment
• Force: accepting questionable orders = +1 Control

=== DEPLOYMENT ===
• Travel (Warp travel = +1 Warp for all aboard, always)
• Planetfall / arrival conditions
• Environment assessment
• First contact with situation
• Force: Warp exposure during travel

=== OPERATION ===
• Primary objective
• Complications (d20 table)
• Combat, investigation, infiltration, diplomacy
• This is where Push decisions happen
• Force: shifts accumulate based on actions

=== EXTRACTION ===
• Getting out alive
• Casualty assessment
• "No one left behind" vs "acceptable losses"
• Pursuit / rearguard
• Force: abandoning allies = +Entropy, heroic rescue = risk

=== DEBRIEF ===
• Report to command (+Control if obedient)
• Heavy losses → +Entropy for survivors
• Force shifts finalized
• Scars assessed
• Downtime (if any — rare in 40K)
```

## RANK

Rank is not a separate track. It's a modifier to Control.

```
RANK:
0: EXPENDABLE — conscript, cultist, ganger, servitor
1: TROOPER — guardsman, initiate, acolyte, battle-brother
2: SPECIALIST — sergeant, tech-adept, veteran Marine
3: OFFICER — lieutenant, interrogator, chaplain, captain
4: COMMANDER — colonel, inquisitor, force commander, chapter master
5: AUTHORITY — lord general, lord inquisitor, sector governor

RANK EFFECTS:
+ Higher Rank = more requisition, authority, access
+ Can issue orders to lower Rank (Guard Orders system)
- Rank 3+: Control rises +1 faster per scene (the system owns you more)
- Disobedience at high Rank: +Entropy equal to Rank (the fall is harder)
- You ARE the system. Breaking free costs more.

RANK AND CONTROL FLOOR:
Control cannot drop below Rank ÷ 2 (rounded up).
A Colonel (Rank 4) cannot have Control below 2.
The machine doesn't let go.
```

## PSYCHIC POWERS

Psykers channel the Warp. The Warp is Hell. Every cast is a controlled disaster.

```
CASTING: d20 + WIS + Psy Rating vs DC

PSY RATINGS:
| Rating | Name       | Bonus | Peril Range | Max Tier      |
|--------|------------|-------|-------------|---------------|
| 1      | Gamma      | +1    | Nat 1       | Minor         |
| 2      | Delta      | +2    | Nat 1       | Minor         |
| 3      | Beta       | +3    | Nat 1-2     | Standard      |
| 4      | Beta-plus  | +4    | Nat 1-2     | Major         |
| 5      | Alpha      | +5    | Nat 1-3     | Major         |
| 6+     | Alpha-Plus | +6    | Nat 1-4     | Apocalyptic   |

EVERY CAST = +1 Warp (minimum). Psychic power IS Push (Warp).
Can Push additionally: +1d6 to casting, +1 more Warp (total +2).

POWER TIERS:
Minor (DC 10): sense emotions, spark flame, minor telekinesis
  Damage: d6 | Duration: 1 minute
Standard (DC 14): telepathy, force push, pyro bolt, precog flash
  Damage: 2d6 | Duration: 10 minutes
Major (DC 18): dominate mind, fire storm, force barrier
  Damage: 4d6 | Duration: 1 hour
Apocalyptic (DC 22+): mass mind control, warp rift, fate manipulation
  Damage: 6d6+ | ALWAYS triggers Perils regardless of roll

WARP SCALING:
At Warp 3+: +1 to casting (power grows)
At Warp 6+: Peril range widens by 1 (Nat 1-2 becomes 1-3, etc.)
At Warp 8+: every cast triggers Minor Perils check automatically
Power grows. Control slips. That's the deal.
```

## FAITH & THE EMPEROR

Faith is real power in 40K. It's also the Imperium's leash.

```
PRAYER: CHA + Faith vs DC

FAITH = faction reputation with the Emperor (0-6).
  At 0: Emperor's silence. No blessings. +1 Entropy per prayer attempt.
  At 1-2: Faint comfort. +1 to next morale save.
  At 3-4: Blessed. Reroll, +1 to roll, or resistance to Warp.
  At 5: Act of Faith. Major miracle possible.
  At 6: Living Saint potential. Reality bends around you.

FAITH IS A PUSH (CONTROL):
Every answered prayer = +1 Control.
Miracles at Faith 5+ = +2 Control guaranteed.
The Emperor protects. The Imperium tightens.

CRISIS OF FAITH:
Faith 0 + failed prayer = WIS DC 14 or +1 Entropy.
The silence is deafening. Maybe no one is listening.
```

## COMBAT

Uses gm-skill combat core. 40K additions:

```
COMBAT IS A MEAT GRINDER:
- Bolter hits HURT. Plasma kills. Melta erases.
- Cover is life. Movement in the open = death.
- Superhuman vs human = no contest without heavy weapons.

COVER:
Light cover (rubble, bush): +2 AC
Heavy cover (wall, trench): +4 AC
Fortified (bunker, vehicle): +6 AC
No cover in open ground = enemies get advantage to hit.

SUPPRESSION:
Heavy/automatic fire at area: enemies must WIS DC 12 or cannot advance.
Pinned units: −2 to all actions, cannot move toward source.

COMBAT FORCE RULE:
Combat is a scene. One Force shift per scene applies.
GM determines by HOW the fight was fought:
  CONTROL: disciplined, tactical, followed orders
  WARP: used forbidden means, psychic, daemon-touched
  ENTROPY: mechanical, felt nothing, just survived
```

## QUICK REFERENCE

```
THREE FORCES:
CONTROL   (0-10) → the Imperium's grip
WARP      (0-10) → Hell reaching in
ENTROPY   (0-10) → the slow death of meaning
All three hostile. One shift per scene. Inaction = +Entropy.

THREE CURRENCIES:
FORCE     = strategic (who's breaking you)
CONDITION = tactical (can you fight)
FORTUNE   = insurance (burn your luck)

PUSH:
CONTROL: +1d4, +1 Control — training, doctrine, obedience
WARP:    +1d6, +1 Warp — forbidden power, desperate measures

CONDITION: 0 operational → 1 hurt → 2 wounded → 3 critical → 4 dying
Condition 3+ = Scar (permanent). 5+ Scars = broken. 7+ = unplayable.

FATE: 0-2 by origin. Cheats death once per point.
FORTUNE: 1-3 by origin. Reroll, +2, enemy reroll, −1 Condition. Cannot reduce Forces.

PSYCHIC: d20 + WIS + Psy vs DC. Every cast = +1 Warp.
FAITH: CHA + Faith vs DC. Every answered prayer = +1 Control.

RANK: 0 expendable → 3 officer → 5 authority
  Higher = more resources, more Control pressure.

MISSION: BRIEFING → DEPLOYMENT → OPERATION → EXTRACTION → DEBRIEF
```
