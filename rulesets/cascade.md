# Cascade — Cinematic Dieselpunk Action

**Setting:** Post-apocalyptic dieselpunk, 35 years after "The Signal"
**Tone:** 80s action cinema — Fury Road, Aliens, Predator
**Core:** Humanity SURVIVED and THRIVES. This is not grimdark.

> *"The Signal killed our toys. It didn't kill us."*

## THE WORLD

### Timeline

**1989 — The Signal (Year 0)**
- March 7th: Amateur radio operators detect anomalous signal from beyond Earth. 72 hours of cosmic radiation.
- March 10th: First crystals appear simultaneously — Hokkaido, California, Urals, Congo.
- March 15th: Electronics within 50m of crystals malfunction. Pacemakers fail. Radios emit Signal on loop.
- April: Scientists name it "Cascade Radiation." Infection spreads 3km/week.

**1990-1994 — The Fracturing**
- 1990: Global communications collapse. Satellites die. Power grids fail. 47 aircraft crashes by June.
- 1991: 250 million refugees. EU collapses. US federal government retreats to bunkers.
- 1992: First Chimera contact. Kyoto — something wearing human skin wrong, speaks Japanese backwards.
- 1993-94: California and Texas declare independence. Japan splits into Houses. Africa proves surprisingly resilient.

**1996-1998 — The Nuclear Option**
- China strikes first (Gobi). Crystals shatter then regrow, but nukes buy time.
- USA: 4 strikes (Nevada, Arizona, Oregon, Kansas). Ends federal authority.
- Russia: 12 strikes. Everything east of Urals destroyed. "Russia burned itself to save Europe."
- Total cost: 800 million dead. Infection stabilizes.

**1999-2009 — Dieselpunk Renaissance**
- Pneumatic computers (compressed air, immune to Cascade)
- Diesel-everything replaces electronics
- Crystal-shielded tech (lead-lined Faraday coffins)
- New nations form: American Compact (2001), Shogunate Revival (2003), African Coalition (Lagos Accords 2005)

**2024 — The Now (Year 35)**
- Crystal zones cover 15% of Earth, dormant 15 years
- Tech level: 1940s industrial + 1980s knowledge fragments
- Diesel, propeller aircraft, vacuum tubes, mechanical radio
- World that SURVIVED — cities bustle, airships cross Atlantic, heroes fight crystalline horrors

### The Crystals

Four colors. Four dooms. Each transforms matter, life, and reality differently.

#### Green — Verdant (Изумруд)
The most common. The "safe" one. (Nothing is safe.)
- **Appearance:** Emerald, plant-like formations, warm to touch, smells like rotting flowers
- **Environment:** Accelerates growth, creates crystal jungles, hyper-fertile toxic soil
- **Exposure:** Euphoria → rapid healing → crystal buds in flesh → full transformation
- **Chimeras:** Prowlers (silent predators), Thicket Horrors (vine-wrapped humans), Bloom Mothers
- **Regions:** Africa, South America, Southeast Asia

*"Verdant zones feel welcoming. That's how they get you."*

#### Purple — Void (Бездна)
Reality gets thin where Void crystals grow.
- **Appearance:** Deep violet, impossible geometries, cold, shadows fall wrong
- **Environment:** Spatial distortion, time fluctuations, gravity anomalies
- **Exposure:** Disorientation → memory gaps → phasing → erasure from existence
- **Chimeras:** Flickerers (multiple positions), Hollow Men (empty mimics), The Absence
- **Regions:** Eastern Europe, Siberia, northern Canada

*"Trust nothing. Not even your own footprints."*

#### Yellow — Radiant (Сияние)
The burning. Gives power — at a price.
- **Appearance:** Brilliant gold, aggressive spires, hot, electrical hum
- **Environment:** Extreme heat, glass deserts, lightning storms
- **Exposure:** Sunburn → fever → skin glows → immolation
- **Chimeras:** Scorchers (living furnaces), Glass Walkers (daylight hunters), Furnace Kings
- **Regions:** Middle East, Australian Outback, American Southwest

*"Radiant makes you feel like a god. That's the trap."*

#### Blue — Stillwater (Глубина)
The quiet. The patient. The one that waits.
- **Appearance:** Pale blue, ice-like, smooth waves frozen in crystal
- **Environment:** Temperature drops, sound dampening, frozen seas
- **Exposure:** Calm → emotional numbing → hypothermia → crystal stasis (aware but frozen)
- **Chimeras:** Sleepers (beneath ice), Whisperers (voices with no bodies), Leviathans
- **Regions:** Oceania, Scandinavia, Antarctica

*"It doesn't kill you. It keeps you."*

### Factions

Six ways to live in the Cascade world. Each is a complete society with its own values, economy, and conflicts.

---

#### Nomads (Road Tribes)

The highways belong to them. Clan-based societies built around convoys and the endless road.

**Structure:** Clans of 20-200 members, usually extended families. Led by Road Chiefs — best driver AND best leader survives. Everyone has a role: scouts, mechanics, gunners, traders. Children learn to drive before they learn to read.

**Values:**
- Freedom above all — walls are prisons
- Clan loyalty is absolute (outsiders can earn trust)
- Your vehicle is your identity — maintain it or die
- Hospitality code: share fuel with travelers, expect the same

**Economy:** Escort services for caravans, long-haul transport contracts, salvage and scrap trade, information brokerage (they see everything on roads).

**Conflicts:** Territorial disputes over routes, city-states trying to "civilize" them with taxes, raiders who give nomads a bad name, crystal zones cutting off traditional routes.

*"We don't run FROM anything. We run TOWARD tomorrow."*

---

#### City-States (Settlements)

Fortified communities ranging from a few hundred to tens of thousands. Each one unique, shaped by geography and history.

**Structure:** Governance varies wildly — mayors, councils, warlords, corporations. District-based organization in larger cities. Citizenship often requires contribution: labor, taxes, or military service.

**Values:**
- Stability and order (methods vary)
- Self-sufficiency as ideal, trade as necessity
- Walls mean safety — what's outside is dangerous
- Progress through cooperation

**City Types:**
| Type | Population | Specialty |
|------|------------|-----------|
| Trade Hub | 5,000-20,000 | Commerce crossroads |
| Industrial | 3,000-15,000 | Manufacturing |
| Agricultural | 1,000-8,000 | Food production |
| Fortress | 500-5,000 | Military stronghold |
| Haven | 200-2,000 | Refugee settlement |

**Conflicts:** Resource wars with neighbors, internal politics (who controls the council?), nomad clans demanding "fair" trade terms, crystal creep threatening boundaries.

*"Behind these walls, we remember what civilization means."*

---

#### Stalkers (Artifact Hunters)

They walk into the crystal zones where sane people fear to tread. They bring back wonders — or don't come back at all.

**Structure:** Loose brotherhood, not organization. Solo operators or small teams (2-4 max). Informal hierarchy based on reputation and survival record. Gathering points: specific bars, trading posts, "Stalker towns."

**Values:**
- The Zone respects no one — only skill and luck matter
- Share information about hazards (selfishness kills everyone)
- Never take more than you can carry out
- Dead stalkers' gear is fair salvage (harsh but necessary)

**Resources:** Artifacts, Pre-Signal tech preserved in zones, maps of safe routes, some develop immunity or resistance to crystal effects.

**Economy:** Artifact trade (premium for verified goods), guiding services for researchers, information about zone conditions, chimera hunting contracts.

**Conflicts:** Competition over prime salvage sites, buyers who don't pay or set traps, Crystal Cults seeing them as heretics, governments wanting to control artifact trade.

**The Guilds:**
- **Zone Runner Concordat** — Oldest and largest. Information sharing network, artifact verification seals, mutual aid. Membership means reputation. Betrayal means death.
- **Deep Walkers** — Elite specialists for Core zones. Small, secretive, legendary survival rates. Hire fee equals small fortune.
- **Scavenger Union** — Mass-market stalkers. Quantity over quality. Strip Fringe zones clean, move on. Corps hire them for bulk salvage.
- **The Marked** — Stage 3+ stalkers who refuse treatment. Use their mutations as tools. Distrusted, undeniably effective. "We're already dead. Might as well be useful."

**Artifact Black Market:**

*Buyers:*
- Corps pay top rates for verified goods, ask no questions
- Crystal Cults pay triple for "sacred" artifacts, prefer green/purple
- Collectors (wealthy eccentrics) want unique pieces, price no object
- Governments buy weapons-grade artifacts, deny everything

*Sellers:*
- Stalkers (direct) — best prices, trust required
- Fences (middlemen) — 30% cut, handle verification, safer for both sides
- Smugglers — move goods across borders, specialize in "hot" artifacts

*Markets:*
- **The Bazaar** (location shifts) — monthly gathering, neutral ground, Concordat-protected
- **Nightside** (Atlantic Union) — permanent black market district, bribes keep law away
- **Kowloon Float** (Pacific) — ship-based market, no jurisdiction, anything goes

*Rules:*
- Never sell unverified artifacts — buyers die, your reputation follows
- Never cheat a Concordat member — you'll never work again
- Payment before goods — standard, exceptions get people killed

*"The Zone doesn't care about your plans. Learn its rules or become another anomaly."*

---

#### Sky Clans (Dirigible Crews)

Masters of the air. Trading fleets, pirate raiders, and everything between. Above the crystal zones, above ground-pounder problems.

**Structure:** Ship = community (crews of 20-100). Captain's word is law, but mutiny happens. Fleet alliances for major trade or conflict. Skyports: neutral ground, no fighting in the docks.

**Values:**
- The ship comes first — always
- Altitude is freedom (ground-dwellers are "dusters")
- Reputation is currency — break your word, lose everything
- Weather-sense is nearly religious

**Ship Classes:**
| Class | Crew | Role |
|-------|------|------|
| Scout | 5-10 | Recon, courier |
| Trader | 20-40 | Commerce |
| Carrier | 50-100 | Fleet flagship, mobile base |
| Raider | 15-30 | Combat, piracy |

**Conflicts:** Trade route disputes with rival fleets, ground-based anti-air, pirate clans, fuel and lift-gas supply issues.

*"From up here, your borders mean nothing. The sky belongs to those bold enough to claim it."*

#### Sea Nations (Ship-Cities)

The oceans never stopped being highways. Some communities stopped touching land entirely.

**Structure:** Flotillas of connected vessels forming floating towns. Flagship councils or admiral-captains. Permanent docks at coastal city-states through trade agreements.

**Values:**
- The sea provides — take only what you need
- Ships are sacred (destroying one is ultimate crime)
- Outsiders welcome if they contribute
- The horizon calls — staying too long in one place is weakness

**The Leviathan Problem:** Sea chimeras changed ocean travel forever.
| Threat | Behavior | Countermeasure |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| Swarmers | Schools attack hulls | Nets, depth charges |
| Lurkers | Ambush predators | Sonar, scouts |
| Leviathans | Territorial, predictable | Avoid or tribute |
| Krakens | Intelligent, vindictive | DO NOT ENGAGE |

**Conflicts:** Territorial disputes over fishing grounds, port fees from coastal cities, pirate flotillas, Leviathan migrations.

*"Land-dwellers build walls against the world. We made the world our home."*

#### Corporations (Industrial Remnants)

The megacorps didn't die — they adapted. Smaller, meaner, still holding pre-Signal contracts and patents.

**Structure:** Board of Directors or single CEO. Corporate enclaves: fortified compounds with pre-Signal infrastructure. Security forces rival small armies.

**Values:**
- Contracts are sacred (enforcement is profitable)
- Pre-Signal knowledge is power
- Expansion through acquisition, not conquest
- Long-term planning (they were here before, they'll be here after)

**Corporate Sectors:**

|--------|-----------|----------|
| Energy | Fuel refinement | Everyone needs it |

**The Major Players:**

- **Volkov Fuel Trust** (Russia) — Family monopoly over post-Strike reserves. "We burned Siberia. We earned what remains."

**Corporate Wars:**
- Bayer-Roche vs Lagos Biomedical — price wars, patent fights, sabotage teams
- Galveston vs Volkov — who dictates global fuel prices?
- Crystal Care — hunted by all corps, too useful to destroy, too dangerous to ignore

**Conflicts:** Hostile takeovers (literal combat), independent settlements rejecting "contracts," artifact hunters with tech corps want, governments trying to nationalize.

*"Civilization requires organization. We provide both — at competitive rates."*

### Life After The Signal

Thirty-five years since the Cascade. The crystals fell, electronics died, and humanity stumbled — then got back up, dusted off, and kept going. The old world's convenience is gone, but people are resilient bastards.

**Communication:**
- Courier networks span continents (bonded messengers, guaranteed delivery)
- Semaphore towers for urgent news (line-of-sight chains)
- Short-wave radio works (long-range scrambled near crystal zones)
- Carrier pigeons still fly — trained birds are valuable assets
- Rumors spread faster than official news (bars are information hubs)

**Travel:**
- Diesel vehicles rule the roads
- Armored convoys for dangerous routes
- Dirigibles for long-haul cargo and passengers
- Coastal shipping never stopped — just got more interesting
- Rail works where tracks remain intact

**Information:**
- Newspapers printed on mechanical presses
- Town criers in settlements
- Bulletin boards at trade posts
- Libraries are treasures (literacy is valuable)
- Maps constantly updated as crystal zones shift

**Medicine:**
- Pre-Signal pharmaceuticals worth fortunes
- Herbalism made a comeback
- Surgery by lantern light, brutal but effective
- Antibiotics are ultimate luxury
- Crystal artifacts for healing (risky but effective)

**Daily Life:**
People wake with the sun, work hard, trade what they have. Communities formed around resources — water, fuel, defensible ground. Bars are information hubs. Entertainment is live: music, storytelling, bloodsport in rougher places. Life isn't grimdark — it's hard, but people laugh, love, build, dream.

**Power Structures:**
| Old Structure | New Reality | Power Basis |
|---------------|-------------|-------------|
| Federal governments | Collapsed/minimal | — |
| State/Regional | City-States, Territories | Control of resources |
| Corporations | Remnants, Cartels | Pre-Signal tech, contracts |
| Military | Warlords, Militias | Firepower, discipline |
| Religious | Cults, Faith communities | Hope, meaning |
| Criminal | Gangs, Syndicates | Trade routes, violence |

**Three Types of Authority:**
1. **Gun Law** — Whoever has the biggest guns makes the rules. Common in frontier zones.
2. **Trade Law** — Whoever controls resources sets terms. Water barons, fuel lords, food cartels.
3. **Community Law** — Consensus, councils, elected leaders. Works in stable settlements.

Most places mix all three. A smart warlord controls the fuel, lets traders operate, and keeps the community fed enough to not rebel.

## MECHANICS

### Adrenaline

Adrenaline measures momentum — how "in the zone" you are. The more action you take, the more powerful you become. Stand still? You're just another survivor.

**Range:** 0-5. Start each session at 1.

#### Gaining Adrenaline (+1)
- Deal or take damage in a scene
- Attempt something reckless ("That's bold")
- Critical Success (margin +10)
- Vehicle mayhem — chase, ram, stunt
- Save someone at personal risk
- Dramatic entrance
- Walk into obvious danger voluntarily

*Max one gain per trigger type per scene.*

#### Losing Adrenaline (-1)
- Extended rest (sleep, downtime)
- Planning more than 2 rounds without action
- Choosing cautious option when bold exists
- Scene transition to non-action
- Critical Failure (margin -10)
- Fleeing a winnable fight

#### Adrenaline Levels

| Level | Name | Effect |
|-------|------|--------|
| 0 | Cold | -2 Initiative. You're just a person. |
| 1 | Warm | Baseline. No bonus, no penalty. |
| 2 | Rolling | +1 to attack rolls. |
| 3 | Hot | +1 to ALL rolls. Ignore first wound penalty. |
| 4 | On Fire | +2 to ALL rolls. Ignore all wound penalties. One reroll per scene. |
| 5 | UNSTOPPABLE | +3 to ALL rolls. Auto-succeed one roll per scene (declare before rolling). |

#### Special Rules

**Adrenaline Surge:** First time you hit 5 in a session, choose one:
- Heal one wound
- Extra action this turn
- Auto-stabilize if dying

**Burnout:** End a scene at 5? Roll d20:
- 1-5: Crash to 1. Disadvantage until rest.
- 6-15: Next scene starts at 4.
- 16-20: No penalty.

**Team Momentum:** If entire party is 3+, assist grants +2 (not +1), group checks use best roll.

## REGIONS

Seven powers shape the post-Cascade world. All thriving — differently.

### THE AMERICAS (American Compact)

The frontier never died — it just moved. When the Signal fell and Washington went silent, Americans did what they'd always done: looked west, loaded up, and kept driving. The American Compact isn't a nation — it's an *idea*. Three major powers, a hundred independent settlements, and the endless Dust between them. Here, your word is your bond, your vehicle is your identity, and opportunity waits for anyone brave enough to chase it.

This is the land of road warriors and oil barons, cattle drives and crystal hunts, boom towns and bust towns. The highways are arteries of commerce and conflict. And somewhere out in the Dust, fortunes wait for those with the guts to claim them.

#### Major Powers

**Texas Republic**
The Lone Star flew before the Collapse — it just flies higher now. Texas declared independence in '94 and never looked back. Built on oil, cattle, and a military tradition that predates the Signal, the Republic is the most stable power in the Compact.

- *Capital:* Austin (rebuilt around the old state capitol, walls thirty feet high)
- *Population:* ~4 million across the territory
- *Government:* Elected President, Texas Rangers as enforcement
- *Resources:* Galveston refineries (largest fuel production), Panhandle ranches, Corpus Christi shipyards, Fort Hood/Bliss military stockpiles

Texans are proud, loud, and loyal. Hospitality is sacred — refuse a traveler water and you're outlaw. The Rangers aren't just law enforcement; they're legends. Every kid grows up on stories of Ranger companies holding the line against chimera swarms.

*"Don't mess with Texas wasn't a slogan. It was a warning."* — Ranger Captain Elena Reyes

**California Federation**
Where the Republic looks backward to tradition, the Federation looks forward. California fragmented into city-states after independence, then reunited under the San Francisco Accords in 2008. Now it's a patchwork democracy — chaotic, innovative, and surprisingly effective.

- *Capital:* Sacramento (rotating presidency between major cities)
- *Population:* ~3.5 million across coastal settlements
- *Resources:* Pacific trade routes, Silicon Valley tech salvage (worth fortunes), Central Valley agriculture, geothermal power

Californians embrace change. They were first to develop crystal-shielded workshops, first to establish regular Pacific crossings, first to trade with the Shogunate. Diversity is strength here. The downside? Politics never stop. The Innovators Guild in Oakland reverse-engineers Pre-Signal tech — their pneumatic computing systems are the most advanced in the Americas.

*"In the Federation, we don't rebuild the old world. We build the next one."* — Guild Master Chen Wei

**Atlantic Union**
Old money, old power, new reality. The eastern seaboard cities — Boston, New York, Philadelphia, the rebuilt Carolinas — formed the Atlantic Union in 2003. It's the most "civilized" power in the Compact, which isn't always a compliment.

- *Capital:* Philadelphia (chosen for symbolic resonance)
- *Population:* ~5 million along the coast
- *Government:* Council of Mayors, hereditary merchant families hold real power
- *Resources:* Atlantic shipping, Pittsburgh steel, Baltimore shipyards, Appalachian coal, financial infrastructure

Union citizens value propriety, contracts, and tradition. Class distinctions matter more here than anywhere else. The old families — Vanderbilts, Morgans — still pull strings. The Atlantic Exchange in New York handles inter-regional contracts. Their notary seal means something.

*"Civilization requires standards. We maintain them."* — Councilwoman Margaret Ashworth III

**The Dust**
Everything between the coasts. Everything that isn't claimed. The Dust isn't a nation — it's a condition.

The heartland took the worst of it. Kansas Strike of '97 left radioactive glass fields. Crystal zones spread across the Great Plains. But the Dust isn't empty. It's *opportunity*.

- Independent settlements — hundreds of them, each unique
- Nomad clans running the highways
- Boom towns wherever resources appear
- Stalker camps at zone edges
- Raiders who don't play nice
- The Burned Lands — nuclear glass fields, still hot

**Major Routes:** I-70 (Battle of the Dust cleared it in 2022), I-40 (southern, Radiant dangers), I-80 (northern, winter closures), Route 66 (legendary, half-myth).

**The Compact Charter (2001):** Not a government — an agreement. Mutual defense against chimera swarms. Shared currency (fuel chits). Free passage for registered traders. Beyond that? Handle your own business.

*"The Dust doesn't care about your flags. It just asks what you're made of."* — Road Chief Billy Crow

#### Culture & Daily Life

**The Road Is Life.** Convoy culture dominates. Your vehicle says who you are: patched junker = desperate, chrome war rig = successful and dangerous, fuel-efficient runner = smart.

**Entertainment:** Roadhouse circuits (fighting, music, gambling), radio dramas ("Tales from the Dust" every Saturday), rodeos with chimera-hunting competitions, death rallies in the Dust.

**Values:** Self-reliance (handle your problems), your word is bond (reputation is everything), hospitality (travelers get water and a meal), second chances (everyone's running from something).

**The Compact Dream:** Show up, work hard, build something. Boom towns appear overnight when someone strikes fuel or crystal. Legends spread of stalkers who walked into the Dust broke and walked out rich.

#### Threats

**Crystal Zones:**
- *Nevada Glass Sea* — nuclear + Yellow. Extreme artifacts.
- *Kansas Scar* — Void crystals, time doesn't work right
- *Ozark Bloom* — Green jungle spreading from Arkansas
- *Great Lakes Stillwater* — Blue crystals freezing the waters

**Chimeras:** Glass Walkers (daylight hunters), Scrapjaws (car-part hybrids), Dust Devils (can't be fought, only fled), Prowlers (silent predators)

**Human Threats:** Raiders, Road Wolves (organized bandits), Fuel Pirates, Compact Conflicts (Texas vs California proxy wars)

**The Burned Lands:** Four nuclear strikes left scars that won't heal. Glass fields glow at night. Pre-Signal bunkers wait beneath, sealed and untouched.

#### Adventure Hooks

1. **I-70 Run** — First convoy through Kansas in 25 years. Settlements cut off since the Strike. Who survived, and how? *"History books or obituaries — one way or another, they'll remember us."*

2. **Silicon Ghosts** — Race to recover intact Pre-Signal server farm in San Jose ruins. Someone else wants it too. Race through Radiant territory to reach the site first.

3. **Petroleum War** — Someone's manipulating Houston fuel prices. Three Compact powers pointing fingers. Find the real culprit before economic pressure becomes shooting war.

4. **Ghosts of Route 66** — Settlements that welcome travelers, feed them well — and those travelers never arrive. A wealthy merchant lost his daughter. He's paying premium for answers.

*"The old America's dead. This America's meaner, leaner, and runs on high-octane. I'll take it."*

### JAPAN (Shogunate Revival)

When The Signal struck, Japan's dense electronics infrastructure collapsed catastrophically — but its cultural memory proved unbreakable. Within a decade, the old zaibatsu families had reorganized into Seven Houses, each claiming descent from historical domains. The Emperor remains a ceremonial figurehead in Kyoto, blessing the Shogun's Council that actually governs.

Japan thrives through precision engineering, fierce loyalty codes, and an artisan culture that transformed dieselpunk necessity into elevated craft. The islands produce the world's finest mechanical components, their diesel-samurai enforce brutal but fair justice, and their shipyards launch vessels that dominate Pacific trade routes.

#### The Seven Houses

**House Matsudaira** — Kanto Plain (Tokyo ruins to Nagoya)
Industrial powerhouse. Controls the largest diesel foundries and munitions factories. Their samurai wear heavy mechanized armor and carry rotary cannons. Pragmatic, business-minded, often accused of valuing profit over honor.

**House Shimazu** — Kyushu
Naval supremacy and shipbuilding. Their diesel-battleships patrol from Korea to the Philippines. Aggressive expansionists who believe Japan must secure overseas resources. Traditional warrior culture runs deepest here.

**House Date** — Tohoku (Northern Honshu)
Agricultural heartland and cavalry tradition. Breed the finest horses, produce most of the rice. Their mounted diesel-lancers are legendary. Considered rustic by southern houses but fiercely independent.

**House Mori** — Chugoku and Inland Sea
Merchant princes controlling inter-island trade. Run the banking systems and commodity exchanges. Prefer negotiation to warfare, maintain extensive spy networks.

**House Uesugi** — Hokuriku (Japan Sea coast)
Crystal researchers and mystics. Their territory borders the largest crystal zone (former Niigata). Study Chimeras, develop countermeasures, rumored to experiment with crystal integration. Distrusted but essential.

**House Tokugawa** — Tokai (Shizuoka to Mie)
The "neutral" house holding the Shogun's seat. Maintains order through careful balance of power. Controls the Imperial Highway network and communication systems.

**House Hosokawa** — Shikoku Island
Artisan masters and cultural preservers. Produce the finest swords, precision instruments, and mechanical components. Small but universally respected; attacking them is considered dishonorable.

"Japanese-made" carries weight across the Pacific — their diesel engines run cleaner, their blades hold sharper edges, their instruments measure finer tolerances.

#### Culture & Daily Life

**Diesel-Samurai** serve each House as warriors and administrators. They carry traditional katana alongside diesel-powered equipment — pneumatic striking assists, armored gauntlets, compressed-air grappling systems. Their code adapts bushido to modern needs: loyalty to House, protection of craftsmen, death before dishonor. Disputes follow strict dueling protocols witnessed by neutral judges.

**Artisan Guilds** hold extraordinary social power. A master swordsmith or precision machinist commands respect equal to minor samurai. Guild secrets pass through apprenticeship systems lasting fifteen years or more.

**Common Life:** Rice farming continues traditional patterns; factory workers follow rigid schedules with lifetime employment guarantees. Kabuki theaters pack crowds, diesel-powered puppet shows tour villages, sumo tournaments determine regional pride. Gambling remains officially forbidden but thrives in every port district.

**Women:** Formally excluded from samurai ranks but run many businesses, serve as guild masters, and occasionally lead households as regents. Female diesel-mechanics increasingly common and respected for precision work.

#### Threats

**The Sado Crystal Zone:** Former Niigata prefecture hosts Japan's largest infection. Crystal formations spread across Japan Sea coast, spawning aquatic and amphibious Chimeras that raid fishing villages. House Uesugi maintains defensive line but struggles to contain expansion.

**Sea Chimeras:** Crystal-infected whales that ram ships, swarms of luminescent jellyfish that corrode hulls, and something massive lurking in the Japan Trench that even House Shimazu's battleships avoid.

**Inter-House Tensions:** Current Shogun grows old without clear succession. Matsudaira and Shimazu position for dominance while smaller houses form defensive alliances. Open warfare unlikely but assassination, economic warfare, and "bandit" raids grow common.

**The Ronin Problem:** Masterless samurai displaced by house conflicts sell their skills across Asia or turn to banditry. Some form mercenary companies; others pursue vendettas spanning decades.

**Foreign Pressure:** Pacific Confederation seeks favorable trade terms while Korean warlords probe Shimazu patrols. Some houses consider foreign alliances — a violation of Shogun's Council edicts.

#### Adventure Hooks

1. **Forgemaster's Daughter** — House Hosokawa's greatest swordsmith has died, leaving techniques only to her daughter — who fled to mainland with a foreign lover. Three Houses want her retrieved; one wants her dead. Find her in coastal China chaos before anyone else does.

2. **Sado Expedition** — House Uesugi hires outside specialists (deniable if killed) to penetrate deep into crystal zone and recover research equipment. What they find suggests someone has been *feeding* the growth.

3. **Tournament of Steel** — Quinquennial mechanical combat tournament in Osaka. A House Matsudaira favorite has been sabotaged. Uncover the conspiracy while navigating gambling syndicates, corporate espionage, and old vendettas.

4. **Ronin's Honor** — Dying ronin begs you to deliver ancestral blade to estranged daughter, now high-ranking House Shimazu officer. The journey reveals he was exiled for refusing to massacre civilians — and the officer who ordered it commands the daughter's fleet.

### AFRICA (African Coalition)

When the Signal hit, Africa had an advantage most didn't recognize: less to lose. While the developed world watched their digital infrastructure collapse, African nations already operated on diesel, coal, and human ingenuity. No banking crashes when half the continent used cash. No communication blackouts when radio and word-of-mouth never stopped.

The Lagos Accords of 2005 formalized what had emerged organically: the Pan-African Coalition. Not a single government — nobody wanted that — but a continental mutual defense and trade agreement with real teeth. Today, the Coalition is the industrial heart of the post-Cascade world. Nigerian refineries supply fuel to three continents. Kenyan dirigibles dominate trans-African and Indian Ocean trade. South African foundries produce the steel that builds everything.

The irony is not lost on anyone: Africa, long exploited for resources, now controls the resources that matter most.

#### Major Powers

**Federal Republic of Nigeria**
The oil giant. Lagos is the largest functioning city in the post-Cascade world — three million people, sprawling refineries, dockyards that never sleep.
- Kaduna-Lagos pipeline = continent's jugular vein, guarded by best military
- "Lagos Special" diesel burns cleaner and longer — formula guarded jealously
- Dominates West African trade, Gulf of Guinea shipping
- Federal Council governs: democratic tradition + pragmatic strongman politics

Nigerian petrochem engineers are in demand everywhere.

**East African Federation (Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda)**
The sky belongs to them. Mombasa Dirigible Yards build the finest airships in the world — reliable, efficient, beautiful.
- Control Indian Ocean trade routes
- Goods from Arabia, Pacific, South Asia flow through Mombasa and Dar es Salaam
- Nairobi = diplomatic heart, neutral ground where disputes settle over coffee

Their approach differs from Nigeria's brute-force industrialism. East Africans value craft, elegance, doing things right. A Kenyan engineer would rather build one perfect airship than ten adequate ones.

**South African Industrial Combine**
Steel, chrome, copper — if it's metal, South Africa mines it, refines it, exports it.
- Johannesburg-Pretoria corridor produces more raw tonnage than anywhere on Earth
- Corporate federation — democracy limited to shareholders
- Workers have unions, unions have power, strikes can shut down half continent's steel
- Supplies Coalition military hardware: tanks, artillery, armored trains. Not subtle, but they work.

**Sahel Confederation (Mali, Niger, Chad)**
The buffer zone. Formed defensive alliance out of necessity. The Sahara's edge is Green crystal territory.
- Best crystal hunters in Africa — navigate Verdant zones that would kill European stalkers
- Produce most purge drugs and anti-infection gear
- Timbuktu = unlikely medical research center
- Poor in industrial terms but rich in expertise

When the Coalition needs something retrieved from a crystal zone, they call the Sahel.

#### Culture & Daily Life

**Ubuntu Mechanics** — humanity through engineering. The old colonial inferiority complex is dead. Africans built this. Not Europeans, not Americans, not Asians: Africans. That pride radiates through everything.

Cities bustle with industry and commerce. Lagos never sleeps. Mombasa's skyline glitters with dirigible masts. Johannesburg's hammers ring through the night. Markets overflow with goods from across the world — African markets, where African traders set the prices.

**Fashion:** Kente cloth work jackets, Maasai-inspired flight suits, Zulu-pattern steel jewelry. Music blends tribal rhythms with machinery — "foundry jazz" from Johannesburg, "diesel highlife" from Lagos. Every major city has fighting pits where diesel-powered exoskeletons clash.

**Education matters intensely.** Coalition funds technical schools across continent. Skilled engineer worth more than gold. Children compete for apprenticeships. Literacy rates higher than pre-Cascade — reading blueprints saves lives.

**Religion adapted:** Christian, Muslim, traditional — all blended. Cascade seen as divine test Africa passed. Ancestor veneration mixed with engineering: "What would grandfather build?" Spiritual leaders bless factories and airships.

**Old tribal tensions transformed.** Rivalry now expressed through commerce and engineering achievements rather than violence. When Yoruba and Igbo compete, they compete to build the better diesel engine.

#### Threats

**The Verdant Belt:** Green crystal infection dominates Central Africa. Congo Basin is crystalline jungle — beautiful, deadly, expanding. African Prowlers are larger and faster than northern cousins. Bloom Mothers reach sizes unseen elsewhere. Coalition maintains "burn lines" — controlled monthly burns along entire frontier.

**Green Tides:** Twice yearly with the rains, chimera populations surge from Verdant Belt. Military units deploy, stalkers mobilize, civilians shelter. Most years the line holds. 2019's "Blood Harvest" — a Congregation-class titan destroyed three towns, 4000 dead. The Coalition never forgot.

**"The Ancestors":** Unique to African zones — chimeras that remember. Thicket Horrors that speak in dead relatives' voices. Shamblers wearing grandmother's face. The psychological toll on African crystal hunters exceeds anywhere else. Purge drugs help the body. Nothing helps when you put a bullet in something wearing your father's eyes.

**Internal Politics:** Coalition unified against external threats but fractious internally. Nigeria and South Africa compete for dominance. East Africa plays kingmaker. Sahel resents being junior partners despite holding front line.

#### Adventure Hooks

1. **Mombasa Contract** — Legendary cargo ship ran aground in Verdant zone. Medical equipment worth fortunes. Enter crystal jungle, find wreck, survive whatever's been gestating in the hold for 35 years. Last transmission: "passengers changing."

2. **Foundry Sabotage** — Someone attacking South African steel production. Explosions, contaminated ore, murdered engineers. Investigate boardrooms and factory floors. Trail leads somewhere unexpected — saboteur isn't human anymore.

3. **Green Prophet** — Charismatic leader preaches infection is blessing. Followers deliberately expose themselves. Movement grows. Some transformed followers seem... functional. Intelligent. Controlled. Is he delusional, or has he discovered something?

4. **Trans-Continental Run** — Coalition VIP needs Lagos to Mombasa in 72 hours. Political reasons — the kind that get people killed. Normal routes compromised. Inland path through infected zones, past raiders and rival factions. Three days, 4000 kilometers, half the continent trying to stop you.

### EUROPE (European Mosaic)

The old continent shattered into a dozen successor states after The Signal, but from those fragments emerged something unexpected: a vibrant tapestry of cultures that trade, compete, and occasionally threaten war — but mostly just argue loudly over dinner.

The Alpine passes control overland trade, Mediterranean ports dominate the seas, and everyone claims their wine is best. Crystal zones scar the eastern plains and parts of the Balkans, but the western heartland thrives. Europeans rebuilt their cities with diesel cranes and determination, blending medieval architecture with brutalist functionality. The result is somehow beautiful.

Rivalries run deep — centuries of history don't disappear — but so do trade agreements, marriage alliances, and a shared understanding that cooperation beats extinction.

#### Major Powers

**Alpine Federation**
What began as Swiss neutrality expanded into a confederation spanning mountain passes from the Pyrenees to the Julian Alps. The Federation controls overland trade between Mediterranean and north.
- Vaults carved deep into mountains store wealth of dozen nations
- Alpine engineers pioneered crystal-resistant tunnel construction
- Diesel-powered funiculars move goods through passes
- Multilingual by necessity — pidgin of German, French, Italian, Romansch
- Small but elite military, defensive fortifications make invasion suicidal

**Nordic Union**
Scandinavia unified out of necessity — long winters demand cooperation. Stretches from Iceland to Finland.
- Build the finest diesel ships in the world
- Icebreakers open northern trade routes each spring
- Fishing fleets feed half of Europe
- Harvest amber-class crystals from Baltic shores — inert but prized
- Society emphasizes collective welfare, practical education, dry humor about frozen darkness
- Explorers push into crystal-scarred Russia, mapping safe routes

**Mediterranean League**
Loose confederation of port cities from Barcelona to Athens. Member cities — Barcelona, Marseille, Genoa, Naples, Venice, Dubrovnik, Athens — maintain fierce independence while cooperating on naval matters.
- Diesel galleys patrol for pirates and sea chimeras
- Europe's breadbasket: olive oil, wine, citrus, grain
- Culture celebrates life loudly: festivals, art, public debates, cuisine
- Venice, partially flooded but stubbornly inhabited — symbol of resilience. They simply built higher floors.

**British Confederation**
The Isles went their own way, as usual. Loose confederation of England, Scotland, Wales, Ireland emerged after decade of "disagreements" (small wars, really). Since settled into comfortable bickering.
- British diesel engineering: uglier but more reliable, they claim
- Merchant marine rivals Nordic fleet
- Coal mines fuel much of northwestern Europe
- Tea somehow survived as cultural obsession
- Pubs serve as community centers where deals are struck

**Rhineland Compact**
Industrial heartland of old Germany and Low Countries. Federation of city-states along the Rhine.
- Ruhr steel, Flemish textiles, Dutch agricultural innovation
- Factories produce diesel engines, tools, machinery exported throughout Europe
- Politically fractious — cities argue constantly about tariffs and whose beer is superior
- Unite against external threats

#### Culture & Daily Life

**Cultural Renaissance:** When electronic entertainment died, people rediscovered theater, live music, public discourse. Every major city has amphitheaters — some ancient Roman structures restored, others purpose-built. Literacy rates actually increased; without television, books became precious.

**Art flourishes:** Dieselpunk aesthetics blend with classical traditions. Sculptures incorporate machine parts. The "New Futurists" movement celebrates humanity's adaptation. Fashion mixes practical workwear with pre-Signal elegance.

**Daily life:** Extended families share housing, pooling resources. Markets, not supermarkets, provide food — loud, social affairs. Coffee houses and pubs serve as public living rooms. Diesel trains connect major hubs; roads see merchant caravans.

**Education:** Practical skills alongside classics. A Leipzig student might study Goethe in the morning and diesel mechanics in the afternoon. Universities in Bologna, Oxford, Heidelberg rebuilt their reputations.

**Religion evolved:** Churches became community centers. New syncretic faiths emerged incorporating crystal phenomena. Most Europeans maintain practical spirituality — seen too much strangeness for pure materialism.

#### Threats

**The Eastern Scars:** Purple (Void) crystal zones dominate Polish plains and much of Balkans. Haunted borderland spawns aggressive Chimeras drawn to populated areas. "Scar Wardens" — multinational military force — maintain loose defensive line.

**Political Fractures:** Old grudges don't die. Rhineland and France's remnants argue over Alsace. Mediterranean cities sacked each other within living memory. British internal politics occasionally turn violent. Tensions rarely escalate to full war — everyone remembers extinction — but border skirmishes and trade disputes constant.

**Pirate Coasts:** North African ports fell to warlords who prey on Mediterranean shipping. "Barbary Successors" range from organized states demanding tribute to simple raiders. Kidnapping for ransom profitable.

**Crystal Cults:** Some worship crystals, believing Signal was divine. Most cults harmless but weird. A few seek to "spread the blessing" by sabotaging containment. The Inquisition (secular intelligence organization despite name) monitors activity.

#### Adventure Hooks

1. **Amber Run** — Nordic icebreaker discovered massive amber-crystal deposit in frozen Baltic. Site lies within unknown crystal zone. Secure the claim before rivals arrive — and before whatever lurks in ice wakes up.

2. **Venice Incident** — Venetian diplomat kidnapped en route to Alpine banking summit, along with documents that could destabilize continental economy. Rescue and recover, quietly, before markets panic. Trail leads through three nations and two criminal organizations.

3. **Scar Warden Deserters** — Entire company went AWOL into Polish crystal zone, claiming they found something important. Commander wants them retrieved — or silenced. What did they find? Pre-Signal bunker? New crystal type? Something worse?

4. **Cultural Attache** — Famous British playwright needs bodyguards for continental tour. Simple enough, except someone's trying to kill her, the plays contain coded messages for intelligence networks, and her "assistant" is definitely a spy. For whom remains unclear.

### RUSSIA (Russian Federation)

They burned half a continent to save the world. Ask any Russian — they'll tell you the story with vodka in hand and steel in their eyes.

In 1996-98, twelve nuclear strikes turned everything east of the Urals into glass and ash. Siberia, the Far East, the Pacific coast — gone. 300 million square kilometers sacrificed to stop the Cascade from swallowing Europe. General Dmitri Volkov gave the final order knowing his daughter was in Novosibirsk. He pulled the trigger anyway.

That's Russian history now: the nation that burned itself so others could live.

Thirty-five years later, the Russian Federation stands rebuilt and proud. Western Russia — from Murmansk to the Caucasus, from the Ural barrier to the Baltic — houses forty million hard-bitten survivors. Moscow gleams with rebuilt industry. St. Petersburg runs northern trade routes. The Volga feeds the breadbasket. The people who stayed, who rebuilt, who remember — they're not victims. They're the ones who paid the price.

#### Structure & Power

**The Duma Council:** Democracy survived, barely. Supreme Duma governs from Moscow — 12 regional governors plus the Premier. Elections every 5 years (messy, loud, occasionally violent, but real). Premier Natalya Orlov, former tank commander, has held the chair for eight years. She's earned it.

**The Five Regions:**
| Region | Capital | Population | Character |
|--------|---------|------------|-----------|
| Moscow Oblast | Moscow | 12 million | Industrial heart, political center |
| Northwest | St. Petersburg | 8 million | Trade hub, Baltic access, old culture |
| Volga Basin | Kazan | 10 million | Agricultural, multiethnic, stable |
| Southern | Rostov | 6 million | Cossack traditions, steppe nomads |
| Urals Frontier | Yekaterinburg | 4 million | The Wall, military zone, stalker country |

**The Barrier Corps:** Military arm dedicated to maintaining the Ural Line. 20,000 soldiers rotate through fortifications, monitoring the irradiated wasteland beyond. Not fighting a war — watching for what might crawl out of the glass.

**The Ochota (The Hunt):** Elite chimera hunters, part military unit, part national institution. When something crystalline crosses the Urals, the Ochota responds. Veterans wear crystallized chimera fangs as medals. Highest honor in the Federation.

#### Culture & Daily Life

**The Sacrifice defines everything.** Every child learns the names: Novosibirsk, Vladivostok, Irkutsk, Krasnoyarsk — cities that died so cities could live. Memorial Day (October 15th) is the holiest day of the year. No work. Candles in every window. The names read aloud on mechanical radio.

**Dark humor is survival.** Russians laugh at things that would make others weep. "At least we don't have to worry about Siberian winters anymore — no Siberia!" Gets a laugh every time. Gallows humor, delivered deadpan, often over the third vodka.

**Vodka** remains the social lubricant. Distilleries are essential infrastructure. To refuse a toast is an insult; to drink alone is a tragedy.

**Community runs deep.** Apartment blocks share everything — food, fuel, childcare, gossip. Privacy is a luxury; solidarity isn't optional. When the temperature drops and the diesel runs low, you survive together or not at all.

**The Banya (bathhouse)** tradition continues. Steam, birch branches, ice water, conversation. Business deals happen in banyas. Treaties are signed in banyas. Every settlement has one.

**Music and literature** flourish. Russians write poems about the Sacrifice, compose operas about the Ochota, paint murals of Volkov's Last Order. Culture isn't a luxury — it's how they process what they survived.

#### Russian Specialties

**Cold-Weather Expertise:** Nobody does winter better. Russian insulated gear, diesel heaters, survival techniques — global standard. When stalker expeditions head into Scandinavian or Canadian zones, they buy Russian.

**Chimera Hunting:** The Ochota wrote the manual. Literally — "The Bestiary of the Cascade" is a Moscow publication, updated annually. Russian hunters study creatures like scientists, fight them like soldiers, respect them like worthy enemies.

**Diesel Engineering:** Heavy machinery, military vehicles, industrial equipment. Russian factories produce the toughest, most repairable engines in the world. Not elegant, not efficient, but DURABLE. A Russian truck will run on motor oil, cooking grease, and pure spite.

**Rasputin Serum:** Russian pharmaceutical research produces the best anti-exposure drugs outside corporate labs. The secret? Test subjects volunteer. The Urals provide endless opportunities to study crystal effects firsthand.

#### Threats

**The Glass Wastes:** Beyond the Urals lies hell. Twelve nuclear strikes didn't stop the Cascade — they created a new horror. Irradiated crystal zones mutated by nuclear fire. The glass glows at night. Things move in the wasteland that don't match any known classification. The Barrier Corps watches. Sometimes they fire artillery at shadows.

**Void Contamination:** Purple crystals dominate eastern zones. Reality warps. Time slips. Stalkers report "echo zones" — places where you hear your own voice from yesterday, where your reflection moves wrong. The Hollow Men sometimes wear faces of people who died in the strikes.

**The Creep:** Despite the nuclear barrier, crystal growth continues. Slowly, millimeter by millimeter, zones expand westward. Yekaterinburg will be Fringe zone within twenty years at current rates. The Duma debates solutions. Nobody has good ones.

**Internal Tensions:** Not everyone agrees on leadership. Cossack separatists in the south remember their independence. Moscow's authority thins at the edges. Some whisper that the Duma hoards resources while outer regions starve. Not quite true. Not quite false either.

**The Volkov Cult:** Some worship General Volkov as a saint who sacrificed his own blood to save humanity. Others call him a mass murderer who killed 300 million. The truth? He was a man who made an impossible choice. But cults don't deal in truth.

*"We lit the match that burned half the world. We'd do it again. That's not cruelty — that's love. You burn what you must to save what you can."* — Premier Natalya Orlov, Memorial Day Address

#### Adventure Hooks

1. **Last Broadcast** — Mechanical radio station picks up transmission from Vladivostok. That's impossible — ground zero for Strike Seven. But someone is broadcasting, repeating coordinates, asking for rescue. Ochota wants volunteers. Hazard pay excellent. Survival odds not discussed.

2. **Defector's Cache** — Former Soviet scientist, dying of exposure sickness, claims he buried research notes — treatment that could reverse Stage 3 transformation. Trade: transport to a specific grave in the Fringe zone. His wife and daughter are buried there. He wants to say goodbye.

3. **Hollow General** — Chimera sightings near Yekaterinburg describe Void creature wearing military uniform, Soviet-era medals still pinned to crystallized flesh. It speaks. It gives orders. Soldiers swear it's General Volkov himself. The Duma wants this handled quietly. Very quietly.

4. **Winter Convoy** — Volga settlement needs emergency diesel before the freeze. Usual route blocked by chimera migration. Only alternative runs through moderate Void zone. Payment: enough fuel to run a settlement for a year, plus salvage rights to anything found en route.

### ARABIAN LEAGUE

When The Signal killed electronics, the world remembered what the Arabian Peninsula had always known: oil is power. Within a decade, the great refineries roared back to life, their diesel-powered crackers and distillation towers rebuilt with 1980s engineering knowledge and 1940s mechanical grit. Now the Arabian League stands as the fuel depot of the rebuilt world — a confederation of city-states, trading houses, and refinery-fortresses that controls the lifeblood of diesel civilization.

Caravans of tanker-trucks crawl across the desert highways. Supertankers — retrofitted with coal-fired boilers — carry precious fuel to Europe, Africa, and Asia. The crossroads of three continents has become the crossroads of humanity's survival.

#### Major Powers

**The Refinery Emirates (Arabian Peninsula)**

The great oil fields of the Gulf never stopped pumping — they just needed new masters who understood mechanical extraction. The Refinery Emirates rose from the ashes of the old Gulf states, a loose confederation of city-states built around massive diesel-powered extraction and refining complexes.

Abu Khalid, Dubai Restored, and the Bahrain Foundries compete and cooperate in equal measure. The Emirates control roughly 40% of the world's accessible refined fuel, and they know exactly what that's worth.

Their cities gleam with brass and chrome, air-conditioned by massive evaporative systems, towers connected by covered walkways to escape the brutal sun. Wealth here is displayed in fuel — the truly rich light their courtyards with burning gas flares, a declaration that they have oil to waste.

**The Persian Highlands (Iran/Mountain Regions)**

While the lowland refineries claimed the oil, the Persian mountain fortresses claimed something equally valuable: water. The ancient qanat systems — underground aqueducts built over millennia — still function, channeling snowmelt from the Zagros and Alboruz ranges to highland cities that never fell.

Isfahan, Shiraz, and the fortress-city of Tabriz maintain their ancient sophistication, their bazaars now trading in precision-machined parts, woven textiles, and the mountain herbs that form the basis of post-Signal medicine.

The Persians style themselves as the intellectuals of the League — their universities continued teaching through The Signal, their libraries preserved. They look down on the "new money" Emirates with ancient pride, but they buy their fuel all the same.

**The Levant Trading Houses (Eastern Mediterranean)**

Beirut, Damascus, and Amman survived The Signal through what they'd always done best: trade. Positioned where Europe, Asia, and Africa meet, the Levant cities became essential middlemen in the post-Signal economy.

The great Trading Houses — some claiming descent from Phoenician merchants — maintain caravans across three continents, their factors and agents present in every major settlement from Gibraltar to Mumbai. They control the overland routes that complement the sea lanes.

The Levant cities are polyglot, cosmopolitan, and ruthlessly commercial — a merchant from the House of Khoury might speak six languages and worship none of them, so long as the diesel flows and the contracts hold.

**The Egyptian Nile Authority (Egypt/Suez)**

Control the Suez Canal, control world trade. The Egyptians understood this immediately. Within five years of The Signal, the Nile Authority had organized the canal's mechanical operation — massive diesel-powered locks and tugs replacing the old electric systems.

Every ship transiting between the Mediterranean and the Indian Ocean pays tribute to Cairo. But Egypt's power extends beyond the canal: the Nile Valley remains one of the world's great agricultural regions. Egyptian grain feeds much of the Mediterranean; Egyptian cotton clothes it.

Alexandria has risen again as a great port, its lighthouse replaced by a massive diesel-powered beacon visible for thirty miles.

#### Culture & Daily Life

**Hospitality then contracts.** A guest in your home is sacred; a business partner is merely bound by law. The great trading families maintain elaborate guest-houses where travelers are fed, housed, and protected regardless of origin. In a world where reputation travels by caravan, a house known for poor hospitality finds its contracts unsigned.

**Desert rhythm.** Work begins before dawn, pauses during the brutal midday heat, and resumes in the evening cool. The great covered souks provide shade and commerce simultaneously, brass-fitted stalls offering everything from machine parts to spices to crystal-hunter artifacts.

**Coffee houses** serve as offices, newsrooms, and social clubs. A man's regular coffee house says as much about him as his family name.

**Wealth displays** have become art. Custom dieselcraft — massive vehicles with chrome fittings and hand-tooled leather interiors. The finest Nile cotton, dyed with Persian indigos, cut in Levantine styles. Women in the League hold considerable economic power, particularly in the trading houses.

**Water remains precious** despite the Persian aqueducts and Nile bounty. Bathing is ritualized, fountains are status symbols, and the deliberate waste of water is the gravest insult.

#### Threats

**The Radiant Expanse (Yellow Crystal Zone)**

The Empty Quarter was already one of Earth's most hostile environments. Now, approximately 30% of it glows with Yellow crystal infection — the Radiant Expanse, where glass dunes sing in frequencies that cause bleeding from the ears and crystal formations grow in geometric patterns that hurt the eye.

The Zone spawns Glass Walkers: humanoid Chimeras whose bodies are fused with crystalline silicon, their touch spreading infection, their movements silent despite their apparent fragility. Caravans skirt the Expanse carefully; those that wander too close rarely return.

**Water Wars** — Despite the Persian aqueducts, water remains the League's limiting factor. The mountain fortresses control the headwaters; the lowland refineries need them. Diplomatic tensions simmer constantly, occasionally boiling over into "trade disputes" that look remarkably like small wars.

**Fuel Pirates** — The tanker convoys crossing the Arabian Sea and Red Sea face organized piracy. Operating from hidden coves along the Somali and Yemeni coasts, pirate flotillas in fast diesel launches raid vulnerable tankers.

**The Glass Plague** — A crystalline infection that begins as beautiful silicate patterns on the skin and ends with the victim's internal organs converting to crystal. It spreads through contact with Yellow crystal or Glass Walker fluids. The Persian physicians have developed treatments that slow progression, but no cure exists.

#### Adventure Hooks

1. **The Burning Contract** — A Levantine Trading House hires the party to escort a vital cargo across the desert to Abu Khalid. The cargo: a captured Glass Walker, somehow dormant, needed by Emirates scientists studying crystal immunity. Halfway through the journey, the creature begins to wake — and someone in the caravan is sabotaging the containment.

2. **Water Rights** — A minor aquifer dispute between a Persian highland town and an Emirates refinery-city is about to become a shooting war. Both sides have hired mercenaries. The party is caught in the middle — or perhaps working for a Levantine house that profits from neither side winning.

3. **The Suez Blockade** — Someone has sunk a ship in the Suez Canal, blocking world trade. The Nile Authority is furious; the Emirates suspect Persian sabotage; the Levant traders are hemorrhaging money daily. The party is hired to investigate — and discovers the saboteur may have had excellent reasons.

4. **Songs of the Expanse** — A crystal researcher claims she's decoded the frequencies the Radiant Expanse emits — and they're not random. They're a message. She needs escort into the Zone's edges to set up recording equipment. What she finds there could change humanity's understanding of The Signal forever.

### PACIFIC RIM

The ocean is the highway, and the Pacific Rim has embraced it completely. Where other regions rebuilt on land, the Pacific chose the sea — and thrived. Massive ship-cities cruise between island chains, diesel-powered dirigibles connect distant atolls, and floating platforms harvest everything from fish to kelp-based fuel.

The Signal's crystal zones created the dreaded Stillwater Reefs — areas where the ocean itself seems frozen in crystalline suspension — but the sea folk learned to navigate around them, chart their growth, and even harvest their edges. This is a civilization of motion, trade, and salt spray, where a captain's reputation matters more than any passport and every island has something someone else needs.

#### Major Powers

**Australian Confederation**

The continent-nation serves as the Pacific Rim's anchor point — literally. While much of the interior remains hostile Red Zone desert, the coastal cities from Darwin to Brisbane to Perth form the region's largest industrial base.

Australian diesel refineries, mining operations, and shipyards supply half the flotillas that sail these waters. The Confederation maintains the "Harbor Cities" — massive port complexes where ship-cities can dock for major repairs, resupply, and trade.

They're known for pragmatic engineering, no-nonsense governance, and the legendary "Outback Runners" who brave the interior crystal zones to extract rare minerals. Their motto: "We built before. We'll build again."

**Indonesian Archipelago League**

Seventeen thousand islands, and every one of them matters now. The League operates the most sophisticated inter-island shipping network in the world, with standardized diesel vessels ranging from two-person courier boats to massive cargo haulers.

They perfected the "island chain" model — each island specializes, all islands trade. Bali grows food, Sumatra refines fuel, Java manufactures, Sulawesi builds ships. Their navigators are legendary, able to read currents, weather, and Stillwater boundaries with uncanny precision.

The League's "Harbor Masters" guild controls trade licensing throughout the region, making them politically powerful beyond their military strength.

**Filipino Flotilla Federation**

The true ship-city pioneers. When the Signal hit, Filipino coastal communities didn't wait for land to become safe — they took to the water and never looked back. The Federation operates over forty major ship-cities, some housing ten thousand souls, constantly moving between resource points and trading hubs.

These aren't crude rafts but sophisticated floating towns with desalination plants, hydroponic gardens, and repair facilities. The Federation's "Fleet Families" have developed a unique culture where your ship-clan matters more than your bloodline, and adoption between vessels is common. Their traders reach every corner of the Pacific.

**Coastal China Enclaves**

The mainland fell to massive Red Zone expansion, but the coast survived — barely. Hong Kong, Macau, and Taiwan formed the "Three Harbor Alliance," preserving pre-Signal industrial knowledge and manufacturing capability.

They're the Pacific's technology center, producing precision diesel engines, navigation instruments, and medical equipment that other factions can't match. The Enclaves are crowded, vertical, and intensely competitive, but they produce engineers and technicians that every other power recruits eagerly. Their neutrality is maintained through being too valuable to attack.

**Pacific Islander Network**

From Hawaii to Tonga to Samoa, the island nations formed a loose confederacy based on traditional navigation, sustainable living, and mutual aid. They rejected the industrial model, instead perfecting low-tech solutions: sail-assisted vessels, coral cultivation, traditional fishing, and crystal-zone mapping through oral tradition.

Their "Wayfinders" use methods passed down for millennia, now adapted to navigate Stillwater boundaries. The Network serves as neutral ground, mediators, and keepers of the old ways. Their communities are small but remarkably self-sufficient, and their hospitality is legendary.

#### Culture & Daily Life

**Life moves with the tides.** Ship-city dwellers wake to the gentle sway of their homes, check the day's heading on posted navigation boards, and head to their shifts — fishing, maintenance, trade sorting, or the essential desalination plants. Fresh water is precious; salt skills are mandatory. Every child learns to swim before they walk, and "dryland legs" is a real condition sailors joke about when visiting ports.

**Reputation travels faster than any ship.** A captain who cheats on cargo deals will find every port suddenly "full." A navigator who reads Stillwater wrong and loses a convoy won't work again. Names matter — ship-names, crew-names, the names of the storms you survived. Storytelling is currency; a good tale of Leviathan escape can buy drinks for weeks.

**Dirigible Post** — lighter-than-air craft running regular routes between major points, carrying mail, small cargo, and passengers willing to pay premium. Larger settlements have semaphore towers and signal-lamp networks for urgent messages.

**Food comes from the sea:** Fish, kelp, cultivated shellfish, and the remarkable "float farms" — hydroponic gardens on stable platforms growing vegetables and fruit. Protein is rarely scarce, but spices, coffee, and tobacco are luxury trade goods.

**Universal sport:** Racing. Boat races, swimming races, diving competitions — if it can be timed, the Pacific Rim bets on it.

#### Threats

**Stillwater Zones**

Crystal infection in the ocean creates Stillwater — areas where the sea itself seems frozen in crystalline lattice, neither solid nor liquid, glowing faint blue in darkness. Ships that enter don't sail out. The zones grow slowly, spawn Blue Chimeras, and occasionally "bloom" — rapidly expanding during certain cosmic conditions.

Charting Stillwater boundaries is critical, dangerous work that supports entire navigator guilds.

**Leviathans**

The great Blue Chimeras of the deep, some reaching lengths of sixty meters or more. Leviathans are crystal-infected sea life grown to nightmare proportions — whales, sharks, squid, things that might have once been submarines.

They're drawn to diesel engine vibrations, requiring ships to observe "silent running" protocols near Stillwater zones. Hunting Leviathans is possible with coordinated flotilla attacks, and their crystal-laced bones are incredibly valuable.

**Lesser Blues**

Not every sea chimera is massive. Razorfins are pack hunters — infected fish swarms that move as one organism, capable of stripping a hull's crew in minutes if they breach. "Crawlers" are crystalline crustaceans that attach to ships, slowly infecting the vessel itself. "Drifters" look like jellyfish but trail crystalline tendrils hundreds of meters long.

**Crystal Storms**

Typhoons, cyclones, and "crystal storms" — weather events that carry Stillwater particulates in the rain. A crystal storm can infect exposed crew, foul engines, and leave ships coated in dangerous residue. Storm prediction is imperfect but critical.

#### Adventure Hooks

1. **The Silent Convoy** — A major trade convoy went quiet three weeks ago, last reported near a supposedly stable Stillwater boundary. The cargo was medical supplies worth a fortune, but the real concern is the hundred-plus crew. The insurance syndicate is hiring independent salvage operators: find the ships, determine what happened, recover what you can. But the Stillwater maps show the zone hasn't moved — so what took them?

2. **Leviathan Season** — Every few years, Leviathans migrate through the Coral Sea in numbers. It's catastrophically dangerous and enormously profitable. The Australian Confederation is assembling a hunting flotilla — twenty ships, coordinated diesel-harpoon attacks, crystal bone processing vessels standing by. They need experienced chimera hunters, mechanics who can keep engines running under combat stress, and anyone crazy enough to face sixty-meter monsters for a cut of the proceeds.

3. **The Floating Library** — Rumor speaks of a ship-city that preserves pre-Signal knowledge: books, technical manuals, research data, maybe even working pre-Signal equipment sealed in Faraday storage. The "Library Ship" supposedly moves constantly, accepting only those who bring knowledge to trade. The Three Harbor Alliance wants it found. The Pacific Islander Network says it should stay hidden. And someone is definitely killing people who ask too many questions about it.

4. **Stillwater Bloom** — A Stillwater zone near a major Indonesian shipping lane is showing bloom indicators. If it expands, it cuts a critical trade route and threatens three inhabited islands. The League needs people to evacuate civilians, chart the bloom's progression, and — if possible — figure out why this zone is blooming when predictions said it should be stable for another decade. Someone or something might be feeding it.

## TONE & STYLE

### What This Is

- **Competence porn:** Characters are GOOD at what they do
- **Earned cool:** Every badass thing was earned through sweat and blood
- **Defiant humanity:** We didn't just survive — we THRIVE
- **Stakes without nihilism:** Death is real, but sacrifice MEANS something
- **Cinematic reality:** Physics bend for spectacle

### What This Is NOT

- NOT grimdark misery porn
- NOT slow prestige drama
- NOT cyberpunk (no neon, no hacking)
- NOT steampunk (no brass goggles fashion)
- NOT ironic — plays it STRAIGHT

### Visual Language

**Materials:** Steel, leather, canvas, oil. Everything has weight.
**Colors:** Rust red, army green, naval grey, oil black, brass gold.
**Everything USED but MAINTAINED.**

**Vehicles:** ROAR. Oversized engines, loud, customized. WW2 bomber aesthetic for aircraft.

**Fashion:**
- Work leather, rolled sleeves, practical boots, tool belts
- High-waisted pants, bomber jackets, combat boots with personality
- Salvaged military + personal touches, painted helmets

### Action Rules

1. **Enter late, leave early** — Start close to action
2. **Show competence immediately** — Skills in first appearance
3. **One-liners earn their place** — Pressure release, not comedy
4. **Escalate through set pieces** — Fight on collapsing bridge, not just "a fight"
5. **Sensory overload** — Heat, copper taste, diesel stink
6. **Pacing is breathing** — Tension builds, releases, builds HIGHER
